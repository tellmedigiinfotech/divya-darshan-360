Condensed Information about the Place:

A. MAIN DEITY
  • Lord Shiva (Vishwanath): The primary deity of the Kashi Vishwanath Temple is Lord Shiva, worshipped here as Vishwanath or Vishweshwara (Ruler of the Universe). This Jyotirlinga is one of the twelve holiest shrines of Lord Shiva.
B. OTHER DEITIES (Sub Deities)
  • Maa Annapurna: Goddess of nourishment, an incarnation of Parvati, the consort of Lord Shiva.
  • Kaal Bhairav: A fierce form of Lord Shiva, believed to be the guardian deity of Varanasi.
  • Dhandapani: Another form of Lord Shiva known for his protective and benevolent nature.

Special Days (Festivals and Utsavas): On special occasions, festivals, and utsavas, the temple timings may be extended, and additional rituals and ceremonies are performed. Some notable festivals and their special arrangements include:

Maha Shivaratri:

  • Timings: The temple remains open throughout the night with special poojas and aartis performed.
  • Activities: Continuous chanting of mantras, special abhishekam (anointment) of the deity, and cultural performances.

Shravan Maas:

  • Timings: The temple experiences extended hours during the entire month of Shravan (July-August) due to the high influx of devotees.
  • Activities: Daily special poojas and aartis, increased frequency of abhishekam, and other rituals.

Kartika Maas:

  • Timings: Similar to Shravan Maas, the temple remains open for extended hours throughout Kartika Maas (October-November).
  • Activities: Special aartis and rituals, particularly on the auspicious days of Kartika Purnima and Diwali.

Pradosh Vrat:

  • Timings: Extended hours on Pradosh Vrat days to accommodate additional rituals and increased devotee attendance.
  • Activities: Special poojas and aartis dedicated to Lord Shiva.
Important Tips for Devotees

  • Follow Guidelines: Adhere to the temple's dress code and guidelines to ensure a smooth and respectful visit.
  • Stay Informed: Check the temple's official website or contact the administration for the most current information on timings and special events.

Location- How to Reach/commuting- Flight/Train/Bus/Taxi Stand/Ferry/Port Air- what ever is available?

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple is located in the holy city of Varanasi, one of the most visited pilgrimage destinations in India. Here are the various modes of transportation to reach the temple:

By Air
Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport (Varanasi Airport)
  • Distance from Temple: Approximately 25 km
  • Connectivity: The airport is well-connected with major cities across India, including Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Hyderabad, and Bangalore. There are also international flights from cities like Kathmandu, Colombo, Bangkok, and more.
  • Airport Transfers: Taxis, app-based cabs (like Uber and Ola), and prepaid taxi services are available at the airport to reach the temple.
By Train
Varanasi Junction (BSB)
  • Distance from Temple: Approximately 4 km
  • Connectivity: Varanasi Junction is a major railway hub, well-connected with most parts of India. Trains from cities like Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai, and Hyderabad frequently arrive at this station.
  • Transfers: Auto-rickshaws, cycle-rickshaws, taxis, and app-based cabs are available from the railway station to the temple.
Manduadih Railway Station
  • Distance from Temple: Approximately 6 km
  • Connectivity: Another important railway station in Varanasi with connectivity to various cities across India.
By Bus
Varanasi Bus Stand
  • Distance from Temple: Approximately 4 km
  • Connectivity: Regular bus services are available from nearby cities and towns. The Uttar Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation (UPSRTC) operates buses to Varanasi from cities like Lucknow, Allahabad (Prayagraj), Gorakhpur, and others.
  • Transfers: Auto-rickshaws and cycle-rickshaws are readily available at the bus stand to reach the temple.
By Road
Taxi and Private Cars
  • Connectivity: Varanasi is well-connected by road to major cities in Uttar Pradesh and neighboring states. You can hire a taxi or drive in a private car.
  • Highways: National Highways NH2, NH56, and NH29 connect Varanasi with various parts of the country.
Local Transportation
Auto-Rickshaws and Cycle-Rickshaws
  • Widely available and convenient for short distances within the city.
App-Based Cabs
  • Services like Uber and Ola operate in Varanasi, providing a convenient option for reaching the temple.
E-Rickshaws
  • Environmentally friendly and commonly used for short commutes within the city.
Important Tips
  • Traffic: Varanasi can have heavy traffic, especially during festivals and peak tourist seasons. Plan your travel accordingly.
  • Navigation: The temple is located in a congested area with narrow lanes. It is often easier to reach a nearby drop-off point and walk to the temple.
  • Local Assistance: Local guides and maps are available for navigation, and it's useful to ask locals for directions if needed.

For more detailed information and travel planning, visit the official Kashi Vishwanath Temple website or contact the temple administration.
