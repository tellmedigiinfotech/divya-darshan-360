.Provide Construction/ scientific information about the temple?

The Ranakpur Jain Temple is renowned for its complex and elaborate architectural style typical of Jain temple art. Constructed in the 15th century, this temple is made from light-colored marble and features over 1,444 intricately carved pillars, each uniquely designed so that no two are alike. The structure has a multi-storied layout, with numerous domes, shikharas, turrets, and cupolas, which adds to its grandeur. The temple covers a vast area and is built on a platform, with a distinct four-faced design dedicated to Tirthankara Adinatha, symbolizing the Tirthankara's conquest of the four cardinal directions.
