Opening & Closing Timing of Harmandir Sahib (Golden Temple):

Construction and Scientific Information about Harmandir Sahib (Golden Temple):

  • Architecture: The Golden Temple, constructed in the 16th century, features a blend of Hindu and Islamic architectural styles. The main structure is built on a 67 ft square platform and surrounded by a holy pool (Amrit Sarovar).
  • Materials: The temple is adorned with gold-plated copper covering the sanctum and white marble on the lower sections.
  • Scientific Aspects: The temple complex includes advanced water management systems in the sarovar and earthquake-resistant construction techniques.

Annual Events at the Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib)

The Golden Temple hosts several significant annual events and festivals that attract pilgrims and visitors from around the world. Here are some of the key events:

Gurpurab Celebrations: The birthdays of Sikh Gurus, known as Gurpurabs, are celebrated with immense devotion. The most notable Gurpurabs are:

  • Guru Nanak Dev Ji's Birthday (November): The founder of Sikhism, Guru Nanak Dev Ji's birth anniversary, usually falls in November. The temple is beautifully illuminated, and the celebrations include continuous recitation of the Guru Granth Sahib, kirtan (devotional singing), and langar (community meals).
  • Guru Gobind Singh Ji's Birthday (January): The tenth Sikh Guru, Guru Gobind Singh Ji's birthday, is celebrated in January with similar devotion and grandeur.
3. Martyrdom Days
  • Martyrdom of Guru Arjan Dev Ji (June): The martyrdom of Guru Arjan Dev Ji, the fifth Sikh Guru, is observed in June. Devotees engage in special prayers and kirtans to honor his sacrifice.
  • Martyrdom of Guru Tegh Bahadur Ji (November-December): The martyrdom of the ninth Sikh Guru, Guru Tegh Bahadur Ji, is observed in November or December with prayers and remembrance ceremonies.

Monthly Events at the Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib)

While the Golden Temple primarily hosts several grand annual events, there are also regular monthly events and practices that offer visitors a chance to engage in the temple's spiritual activities. Here are some of the key monthly events:

Special Langar Services: In addition to daily Langar, the Golden Temple organizes special Langar services on significant monthly occasions such as Sangrand and Puranmashi. These services emphasize the Sikh principles of selfless service (Seva) and community support.

For specific dates of these monthly events, you can refer to the Golden Temple events calendar or check with the temple's official announcements.
