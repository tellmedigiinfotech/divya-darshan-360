The Vaijnath Jyotirling Temple, also known as Parli Vaijnath Jyotirling, is an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in Parli Vaijnath, Maharashtra. Here are some construction and scientific details about the temple:

Architecture:

  • The temple is constructed of stone on a small hill and stands approximately 75-80 feet above ground level. The architecture includes a main entrance on the east side, a brass-plated door, corridors, and a courtyard surrounded by four strong walls.
  • The main gate, known as "Mahadwar," is accompanied by a minaret called Prachi or Gawaksha (window). The temple can be accessed via a wide staircase​ (Parli Vaijnath Temple)​​ (Parli Vaijnath Temple)​.

Renovation:

  • The temple is believed to have been built around 3000 years ago. It was renovated in 1706 by Ahalyabai Holkar, the Queen of the Maratha Malwa Kingdom​ (Parli Vaijnath Temple)​.

Materials Used:

  • The primary material used in the temple's construction is stone. The smooth-surfaced Shivalingam inside the temple is made from the Saligram stone, known for its unique texture and religious significance​ (Parli Vaijnath Temple)​.

Scientific Information

Geological Significance:

  • The temple is located on the Balaghat range, which is a geological formation known for its ancient rocks. This location provides the temple with a strong and stable foundation​ (Parli Vaijnath Temple)​.

Historical Legends:

  • According to legend, the temple's Shivalingam contains the hidden essence of Dhanavantari (the physician of the gods) and Amrit (nectar of immortality), which was hidden by Lord Vishnu during the churning of the ocean (Samudra Manthan). This association adds a mythological and possibly geo-mythological significance to the temple's location and construction​ (Parli Vaijnath Temple)​.

Cultural and Religious Importance:

  • The temple is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, which are considered highly sacred abodes of Lord Shiva. The cultural significance of the temple is enhanced by its association with various legends and rituals, such as the unique practice of offering Bilva leaves to Lord Vishnu and Tulsi to Lord Shiva during certain festivals​ (Parli Vaijnath Temple)​​ (Parli Vaijnath Temple)​.

The Parli Vaijnath Jyotirlinga Temple, also known as Shri Vaidyanatha Temple, holds significant historical and religious importance. Here are some key details about the temple's construction and special significance:

Architecture:

  • The temple is built of stone on a small hill and stands approximately 75-80 feet above ground level. It features a brass-plated main entrance gate, spacious corridors, and a courtyard surrounded by strong walls. The architecture is a blend of Hemadpanthi and Indo-Aryan styles, showcasing intricate carvings and sculptures.

Renovation History:

  • The temple has undergone several renovations over centuries, notably by Ahalyabai Holkar in the 1700s. Contributions for its preservation and development have come from various dynasties, including the Chalukya, Yadava, and Bhonsle dynasties.

Scientific and Geological Significance:

  • The temple is situated on the Balaghat range, a geological formation known for its ancient rocks. This provides a strong foundation for the temple structure.

Special Significance

Geomagnetic Field:

  • While there is no specific mention of a geomagnetic field similar to Kasar Devi, the temple's location on the Balaghat range and its ancient construction methods contribute to its unique spiritual atmosphere.

Historical and Mythological Importance:

  • According to legend, the temple is where a piece of the Lingam brought by Ravana fell and became established as a Jyotirlinga. This adds to its divine significance, making it a revered site for devotees seeking the blessings of Lord Shiva.

Religious Significance:

  • The Lingam at Parli Vaijnath is considered a powerful manifestation of Lord Shiva. The temple is an important stop on the pilgrimage route of the twelve Jyotirlingas, known as the Dwadasha Jyotirlinga Yatra. Devotees believe that visiting these Jyotirlingas brings spiritual enlightenment and divine grace.

Festivals and Rituals:

  • Major festivals like Maha Shivaratri, Shravan Somvar, and Kartik Pournima are celebrated with great fervor. Daily rituals include Abhishekam (holy bathing of the Lingam) and Aarti (devotional ritual with lamps).

The temple also holds cultural significance due to its association with both Shiva and Vishnu, reflecting the harmonious blend of different aspects of Hinduism.

Annual Event of the temple day and date?

The Parli Vaijnath Jyotirlinga Temple holds several significant annual events that attract a large number of devotees. One of the major events celebrated at the temple is Tripurari Purnima, which typically falls in November. This festival marks the victory of Lord Shiva over the demon Tripurasura and is observed with grand rituals and festivities at the temple.

Additionally, the temple celebrates Vaikuntha Chaturdashi, a unique festival where both Lord Shiva and Lord Vishnu are worshipped together. This event also involves special rituals and is a time when devotees flock to the temple to participate in the celebrations.

Monthly Event of the temple - day and date?

The Parli Vaijnath Jyotirlinga Temple hosts a significant monthly event known as Vaikuntha Chaturdashi, which occurs on the 14th day of the waxing moon in the Hindu month of Margashirsha (usually in November or December). This event marks the day when it is believed that Lord Vishnu opens the doors of Vaikuntha, his heavenly abode, for devotees.

During this monthly event, special rituals and prayers are performed, and both Lord Vishnu and Lord Shiva are worshipped. Devotees often visit the temple to seek blessings for prosperity and salvation.
