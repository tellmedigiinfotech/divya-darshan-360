.Provide Construction/ scientific information about the temple?

The Rajagopalaswamy Temple in Mannargudi, known for its grand Dravidian architecture, was originally constructed by King Kulothunga Chola over a thousand years ago and has been expanded by various dynasties over the centuries. Its significant features include:

  • Construction and Expansion: Initially built by Kulothunga Chola I during 1070-1125 A.D., the temple saw significant expansions under the Thanjavur Nayaks in the 16th century, who constructed the current temple structure including the hall of 1000 pillars, the main gopuram (gateway tower), and the large compound wall around the temple. These additions have been instrumental in shaping the temple's majestic architectural ensemble.
  • Architectural Style and Features: The temple is a testament to the Dravidian architectural style, characterized by tall gopurams (gateway towers), intricate carvings, and expansive courtyards. The temple complex includes 16 gopurams, 7 prakarams (outer courtyards), 24 shrines, and nine sacred theerthams (temple tanks).
  • Scientific and Cultural Significance: The temple's layout and construction techniques reflect the advanced engineering and architectural skills of its builders. The temple not only serves as a place of worship but also as a cultural hub that preserves and promotes traditional rituals and festivals. Its design facilitates the performance of elaborate rituals and large gatherings during festivals like Panguni Brahmotsavam.
  • Rajagopuram and Temple Tank: One of the standout features is its 192 ft tall rajagopuram, and the Haridra Nadhi, one of the largest temple tanks in India, which is integral to the temple's rituals and celebrations.

This combination of historical significance, architectural grandeur, and religious fervor makes the Rajagopalaswamy Temple a profound symbol of Tamil Nadu's rich cultural heritage​ (Wikipedia)​​ (This Day in Indian History)​​ (RVA Temples)

Annual Event of the temple day and date?

The annual event of the Rajagopalaswamy Temple in Mannargudi is the Panguni Brahmotsavam, which is a major festival celebrated with great fervor. This festival typically occurs during the Tamil month of Panguni, which falls around March to April each year. The celebration lasts for 18 days and is renowned for its grand chariot procession, where a bronze idol of Lord Krishna from the Chola period is taken out for the procession. This event is a vibrant display of devotion, featuring rituals, traditional music, and cultural performances, drawing devotees from various regions​ (Wikipedia)​​ (Trawell.in)​​ (NativePlanet)​.
