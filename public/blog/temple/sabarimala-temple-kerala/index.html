Opening & Closing Timing of Sabarimala Temple

(a) General Timings
  • Opening: 3:00 AM
  • Closing: 11:00 PM

These general timings are followed during the regular pilgrimage season (Mandala Pooja and Makaravilakku season) as well as during the monthly poojas (first five days of each Malayalam month).

(b) Special Days (Festivals and Utsavas)

During special festivals and utsavas, the timings may be extended or modified to accommodate the large number of devotees. Here are some specific examples:

Mandala Pooja (Mid-November to Mid-January):

  • Opening: 3:00 AM
  • Closing: 11:30 PM (or later, depending on the rush of devotees)
2. Makaravilakku (January 14th)
  • Opening: 3:00 AM
  • Closing: Temple remains open longer to manage the heavy influx of devotees. It may stay open until midnight or beyond.
3. Vishu (Mid-April)
  • Opening: 3:00 AM
  • Closing: 11:30 PM

Monthly Pooja (First five days of each Malayalam month):

  • Opening: 3:00 AM
  • Closing: 11:00 PM

General Entry
  • Entry Fees: There is no entry fee for general darshan at Sabarimala Temple. All devotees can enter the temple and participate in the darshan free of charge.
Special Entry and Privileges
  • Special Entry (Quick Darshan):
  • Availability: During peak seasons like Mandala Pooja and Makaravilakku, the temple may offer a special entry pass for quick darshan to manage the large crowd.
  • Fees: A nominal fee may be charged for the special entry pass.
  • Privileges: This pass grants quicker access to the deity, bypassing the longer queues. It may also include access through a special gate or priority viewing area.
  • Special Poojas and Rituals:
  • Booking: Devotees can book special poojas and rituals in advance either online or at the temple counter.

Entry Restrictions and Requirements for Foreigners
  • Dress Code: All devotees, including foreigners, must adhere to the traditional dress code. Men are expected to wear a mundu (white cloth) without a shirt, while women aged 10-50 are traditionally not allowed entry.

  • Declaration: There is no specific declaration required for foreigners, but all devotees must follow the temple rules and guidelines.

Summary

How to Reach Sabarimala Temple

Sabarimala Temple is located in the Pathanamthitta district of Kerala, India. It is a significant pilgrimage site, especially during the Mandala Pooja and Makaravilakku seasons. Here are the various modes of transportation to reach Sabarimala:

By Air
  • Nearest Airport: Trivandrum International Airport (approximately 170 km from Pamba, the base camp for Sabarimala).
  • Alternative Airport: Cochin International Airport (approximately 160 km from Pamba).
  • Travel from Airport: From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Pamba.
By Train
  • Nearest Railway Station: Chengannur Railway Station (approximately 93 km from Pamba).
  • Alternative Stations: Kottayam (approximately 110 km) and Thiruvalla (approximately 100 km).
  • Travel from Station: From the railway station, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Pamba.
By Bus
  • Kerala State Road Transport Corporation (KSRTC): KSRTC operates regular bus services to Pamba from various cities in Kerala, including Trivandrum, Cochin, Kottayam, and Chengannur.
  • Private Buses: Private bus services are also available from major cities to Pamba.
By Taxi
  • Prepaid Taxis: Available from airports, railway stations, and major cities to Pamba.
  • Online Taxi Services: You can book online taxi services through various platforms for a comfortable journey to Pamba.
By Car
  • Self-Drive: If you prefer driving, you can reach Pamba by car. There are parking facilities available at Pamba where you can park your vehicle and start the trek to Sabarimala.
By Ferry
  • Ferry Services: There are no direct ferry services to Sabarimala. The most convenient way is to travel by road to Pamba and then trek to the temple.
From Pamba to Sabarimala
  • Trek: The final stretch to Sabarimala Temple involves a trek of about 5 km from Pamba. The trek is through a forested path and is an essential part of the pilgrimage experience.
  • Dolly Services: For those unable to trek, dolly services (palanquins) carried by porters are available for hire.

Temple Prashad services- Meals- procedure, payments if any, different class/lines? How to buy the ticket, if needed and timings?

Sabarimala Temple offers prasad and meals to devotees, reflecting the temple's tradition of hospitality and devotion. Here’s a detailed guide on the temple's prasad services:

Types of Prasad and Meals
1. Aravana Payasam
  • A sweet pudding made of rice, jaggery, and ghee, considered very auspicious and a must-have for pilgrims.
2. Appam
  • A sweet dish made from rice flour and jaggery, deep-fried and offered to the deity before being distributed to devotees.
3. Free Meals (Annadanam)
  • Sabarimala Temple provides free meals to all devotees during the pilgrimage season as part of the Annadanam scheme.
Procedure and Payments
  • Aravana Payasam and Appam:
  • Tickets: Tickets for these prasad items can be purchased at designated counters at Pamba and near the temple.
  • Cost: Aravana Payasam is typically priced around INR 100 per container, and Appam is around INR 30 per packet. Prices can vary slightly based on administrative decisions.
  • Timings: Available throughout the day as long as the temple is open to pilgrims.
  • Free Meals (Annadanam):
  • Procedure: No ticket is required for the free meals provided under Annadanam.
  • Timings: Meals are usually served during lunch and dinner times, and the timing may extend during high pilgrimage days to accommodate all devotees.
  • Location: Annadanam meals are served in a large dining hall specifically designated for this purpose.
Special Arrangements
  • Different Classes/Lines:
  • While there is generally no distinction in the serving lines for meals, priority may be given to elderly, physically challenged, and monks in separate lines to facilitate easier access.
  • High Season Arrangements:
  • During peak pilgrimage seasons, additional counters and extended service hours are implemented to manage the increased number of devotees.
How to Buy Prasad
  • On-Site Purchase: As mentioned, prasad can be purchased via tickets at counters. These counters are well-marked and located at strategic points to be easily accessible.
  • Online Booking: For convenience, especially during peak seasons, Sabarimala Temple has started offering online booking for Aravana Payasam and Appam. This can be done through the official website of the Travancore Devaswom Board.

Annual Event of the temple day and date?

One of the most significant annual events at Sabarimala Temple is the Makaravilakku festival, which is a major religious celebration attracting millions of pilgrims from around the world.

Event Details
  • Day and Date: Makaravilakku occurs annually on January 14th. This date corresponds with the festival of Makara Sankranti, which is celebrated across India, marking the transition of the sun into the zodiac sign of Capricorn (Makara in Sanskrit).
Significance
  • Makaravilakku is considered a divine phenomenon at Sabarimala. It includes the appearance of the celestial light on the eastern horizon at Ponnambalamedu (the hill facing the Sabarimala shrine) and is believed to be a blessing from the deity.
  • The festival commemorates the day when Lord Ayyappa, the presiding deity of Sabarimala Temple, is said to bless his devotees in the form of this celestial light.
Rituals and Celebrations
  • Ayyappa Idol Adornment: On this day, the idol of Lord Ayyappa is adorned with Thiruvabharanam (sacred ornaments) brought from the Pandalam Palace, where Ayyappa was believed to be born as a human.
  • Special Pujas and Offerings: Devotees engage in special pujas and present offerings to the deity for blessings.
  • Gathering of Devotees: Hundreds of thousands of devotees gather at Sabarimala to witness the holy event, making it one of the largest annual pilgrimages in the world.
Additional Observations
  • Preparation: Due to the large influx of pilgrims, extensive preparations are made by the temple authorities to manage the crowd, ensure security, and provide necessary facilities like food, water, and medical services.
  • Climactic Pilgrimage: The event marks the culmination of the pilgrimage season at Sabarimala, which begins in November and ends in January, following the Makaravilakku.

Monthly Events at Sabarimala Temple: Pradosham

Sabarimala Temple hosts various monthly events and special pujas, with Pradosham being one of the significant monthly observances. This event occurs twice in a Hindu lunar month on the thirteenth day of every fortnight.

Details of Pradosham
  • Frequency: Twice a month, during the waning (Krishna Paksha) and waxing (Shukla Paksha) phases of the moon.
  • Day and Date: The specific dates for Pradosham vary each month according to the lunar calendar. It generally falls one day before the new moon day and the full moon day.
Significance
  • Dedication to Lord Shiva: Pradosham is primarily dedicated to Lord Shiva. Devotees believe that praying to Lord Shiva during this time will result in the removal of sins and the granting of desires.
  • Spiritual Importance: The time during Pradosham is considered particularly auspicious for worshiping Shiva. It is believed that Lord Shiva and Parvati are in a happy state during Pradosham time, making it an ideal time for prayers and meditation.
Rituals and Celebrations
  • Special Pujas: Special rituals and pujas are performed during the Pradosham period, which lasts for about 1.5 hours before and after the sunset.
  • Offerings and Prayers: Devotees offer milk, holy water, and bael leaves to the deity. They also engage in chanting mantras and singing hymns dedicated to Lord Shiva.
  • Circumambulation: It is customary for devotees to circumambulate the main temple or the deity's idol during Pradosham, performing three rounds as a mark of respect and devotion.
Observance at Sabarimala
  • Integration with Ayyappa Worship: Although Pradosham is traditionally associated with Lord Shiva, at Sabarimala, where Lord Ayyappa is worshipped, the event takes on a unique character, blending Shaiva traditions with the worship practices of Ayyappa.
  • Special Arrangements: The temple ensures that all facilities are adequately prepared to accommodate the influx of devotees during Pradosham days, given its significance.
