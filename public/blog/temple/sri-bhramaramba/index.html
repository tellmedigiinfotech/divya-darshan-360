Construction and Scientific Information about Sri Bhramaramba Mallikarjuna Swamy Varla Devasthanam

Architectural Style: The temple showcases a combination of Dravidian and Vesara architectural styles, characterized by intricate carvings and grand gopurams (tower gateways).

Materials Used: Constructed primarily from granite, the temple structure is designed to withstand the test of time.

Significance: The temple is located at Srisailam, which is known for its unique geological and ecological environment, contributing to its spiritual ambiance.

Annual Event

The major annual event at the Sri Bhramaramba Mallikarjuna Swamy Varla Devasthanam is the Maha Shivaratri festival, which is celebrated with great devotion and grandeur. This festival typically falls in February or March, depending on the Hindu lunar calendar. During Maha Shivaratri, the temple witnesses a large influx of devotees who participate in various rituals and special poojas dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Monthly Events

Monthly events at the temple include Pradosham, which occurs twice a month on the 13th day of each lunar fortnight. On these days, special rituals and poojas are performed in the evening to honor Lord Shiva. Additionally, Amavasya (new moon) and Pournami (full moon) days are marked by special poojas and celebrations.
