Provide Construction/scientific information about the temple ?

The Siddhatek Siddhivinayak Ganpati Temple is built on a small hillock and is surrounded by a thick forest. The temple is constructed from black stone and has a unique idol of Lord Ganesha made from black stone as well. The temple features a dome-shaped ceiling and intricate carvings. The structure is ancient and is known for its architectural beauty and spiritual significance.

Special Significance

The Siddhatek Siddhivinayak Ganpati Temple is one of the eight Ashtavinayak temples dedicated to Lord Ganesha. It holds a unique spiritual significance as it is believed that Lord Vishnu acquired Siddhi (spiritual power) here. The temple is also known for its ancient idol of Lord Ganesha, which is believed to be self-manifested (Swayambhu). This adds to its divine and mystical aura, attracting numerous devotees seeking blessings and spiritual upliftment.

  • Ganesh Chaturthi: This is the main annual festival celebrated at the Siddhatek Siddhivinayak Ganpati Temple. It typically falls in the month of Bhadrapada (August-September), according to the Hindu lunar calendar. The festival includes special poojas, rituals, and cultural programs.

Monthly Event of the Siddhatek Siddhivinayak Ganpati Temple

Sankashti Chaturthi

Day: Sankashti Chaturthi is observed every month on the fourth day after the full moon (Krishna Paksha) in the Hindu lunar calendar.

Significance: Devotees fast and perform special poojas to Lord Ganesha. It is believed that observing this fast removes obstacles and brings prosperity.

Pooja Timings: Special poojas are conducted in the evening on Sankashti Chaturthi.
