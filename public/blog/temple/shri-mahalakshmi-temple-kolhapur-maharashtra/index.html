Condensed Information about the Place
A. MAIN DEITY
  • Goddess Mahalakshmi: Also known as Ambabai, the main deity is a representation of Goddess Lakshmi, the Hindu goddess of wealth, prosperity, and power. The idol is made of black stone, weighs about 40 kilograms, and is believed to be around 3,500 years old.
B. OTHER DEITIES (Sub deities)
  • Lord Mahadev (Shiva): A shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva, an important deity in the Hindu pantheon known for his role as the destroyer and transformer.
  • Vishnu: An idol of Lord Vishnu, the preserver among the Holy Trinity of Hindu gods, can be found within the temple complex.
  • Kartikeya: The temple also houses a shrine for Kartikeya, the son of Shiva and Parvati, revered as the god of war.
  • Ganapati (Ganesha): A shrine dedicated to Lord Ganesha, the remover of obstacles and the god of beginnings and wisdom.
  • Navagrahas: The temple complex includes a shrine dedicated to the Navagrahas, the nine planetary deities significant in Hindu astrology.

Architectural Details
1. Historical Construction
  • Era: The Shri Mahalakshmi Temple, also known as the Ambabai Temple, dates back to the 7th century and is believed to have been built during the reign of the Chalukya dynasty.
  • Material: The temple is primarily constructed using black basalt stone, known for its durability and strength.
2. Temple Structure
  • Main Shrine: The sanctum sanctorum houses the main deity, Goddess Mahalakshmi. The idol is made of black stone and weighs around 40 kilograms.
  • Mandap: The temple features a large mandap (hall) with intricately carved pillars and a beautifully designed ceiling.
  • Garbhagriha: The innermost sanctum (garbhagriha) is where the main deity resides, featuring elaborate carvings and sculptures.
3. Architectural Style
  • Dravidian Architecture: The temple follows the Dravidian style of architecture, characterized by its pyramid-shaped tower (vimana) over the sanctum.
  • Sculptures and Carvings: The temple walls and pillars are adorned with intricate carvings depicting various Hindu deities and mythological scenes. These carvings exhibit high craftsmanship and attention to detail.
