Overview

Ranakpur Jain Temple, built in the 15th century, is a stunning architectural marvel dedicated to Tirthankara Adinatha. Known for its intricate marble carvings and extensive complex, it is one of the most significant Jain temples in India.

The Ranakpur Jain Temple, constructed in the 15th century, is a marvel of marble architecture. It features intricate carvings, 1,444 uniquely designed pillars, and a series of domes and spires. The temple is dedicated to Adinatha, the first Tirthankara, and is renowned for its detailed artistry and structural design. The temple’s construction utilized advanced techniques of the time, ensuring both aesthetic beauty and structural stability
