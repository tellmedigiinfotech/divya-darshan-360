.Provide Construction/ scientific information about the temple?

The Guruvayur Temple, dedicated to Lord Krishna, is one of the most important and ancient temples in Kerala. Here's a detailed look at the construction and some scientific aspects of the temple:

Main Structures:

  • Sreekovil: The sanctum sanctorum where the idol of Lord Krishna is placed. It is built in traditional Kerala style with a conical roof.
  • Nalambalam: The inner courtyard that surrounds the sanctum sanctorum.
  • Balikkalpura: The altar for offerings.
  • Koothambalam: The hall for performing arts.
  • Deepastambham: The light tower, often covered with brass or gold plating.
  • Gopuram: The monumental tower at the entrance, decorated with sculptures.

Idol: The main deity, Lord Krishna, is depicted as a child (Bala Krishna). The idol is made of a unique stone called Patala Anjana, believed to have medicinal properties and the ability to purify the surroundings.

Scientific Aspects

Scientific Healing: The idol of Lord Krishna is made of Patala Anjana Stone, which is known for its medicinal properties. Devotees believe that the very presence in the temple and sight of the idol can have healing effects.

Historical and Cultural Significance

Cultural Hub: Guruvayur Temple is not only a place of worship but also a cultural center where traditional art forms like Kathakali, Carnatic music, and other classical dances are regularly performed.

For more detailed information, architectural plans, and scientific research related to the temple, it is advisable to consult scholarly articles, official publications from the Guruvayur Devaswom Board, or directly contact the temple administration through their official website Guruvayur Devaswom.

Yes, the Guruvayur Temple in Kerala holds special significance, both spiritually and historically. While it may not have the same type of geomagnetic or cosmic energy significance as the Kasar Devi Temple in Uttarakhand, it is revered for several unique attributes:

Spiritual Significance

Lord Krishna's Presence:

  • The temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna, who is worshipped here as Guruvayurappan. The idol is believed to be over 5,000 years old, making it one of the oldest and most significant representations of Lord Krishna in India.
  • It is said that the idol was worshipped by Lord Krishna’s parents, Vasudeva and Devaki, and was later brought to Kerala by Guru (Brihaspati) and Vayu (the wind god), hence the name Guruvayur.

Miraculous Powers:

  • Devotees believe that the temple and the deity possess miraculous powers. Many stories of healing, fulfillment of wishes, and divine interventions are associated with the temple.
  • The temple is also considered an important center for Bhakti (devotional worship), attracting millions of devotees seeking blessings and spiritual solace.

Cultural Hub:

  • Guruvayur Temple is a center for classical arts, particularly Carnatic music and traditional dance forms like Kathakali. It hosts numerous cultural events and festivals, contributing to the preservation and promotion of Indian culture and heritage.

Architectural and Historical Significance

Unique Idol:

  • The idol of Lord Krishna is made from a sacred stone called Patala Anjana. This stone is believed to have special properties and is considered extremely holy.
  • The idol's posture and features are unique, depicting Krishna as a child with a conch, a discus, a mace, and a lotus.

Ancient Temple:

  • The temple is said to have been built by Vishwakarma, the divine architect, in the traditional Kerala style. It has stood the test of time and continues to be a major pilgrimage site.

Connection to Mahabharata:

  • According to legend, the idol at Guruvayur was worshipped by Lord Krishna's parents and was brought to Kerala by the sage Parashurama. This historical connection to the Mahabharata period adds to the temple’s significance.

Festivals and Rituals

Ekadasi Festival:

  • The Vrishchika Ekadasi is the most important festival celebrated at Guruvayur. It marks the day when Lord Krishna revealed the Bhagavad Gita to Arjuna. The temple sees a massive influx of devotees during this time.

Chembai Sangeetholsavam:

  • This annual music festival held at the temple honors the late Carnatic music maestro Chembai Vaidyanatha Bhagavatar. It is one of the largest music festivals in South India.

The religious rituals, the sanctity of the place, and the collective devotion of millions contribute to the temple's powerful spiritual ambiance.

Annual Event of the temple day and date?

Guruvayur Temple hosts several important annual events and festivals that attract devotees from all over the world. The most significant ones include:

Guruvayur Ekadasi:

  • Date: This festival is observed on the Ekadasi (11th day) of the waxing moon in the Malayalam month of Vrischikam (November-December).
  • Significance: Guruvayur Ekadasi is considered the most auspicious day at the temple. It marks the day when Lord Krishna revealed the Bhagavad Gita to Arjuna. The day is filled with special rituals, offerings, and a grand procession.

Chembai Sangeetholsavam:

  • Date: This annual music festival starts on the 14th of November and lasts for about 11-15 days.
  • Significance: It is one of the largest Carnatic music festivals in India, held in honor of the late Chembai Vaidyanatha Bhagavatar. Musicians from all over the country participate in this grand event.

Ulsavam (Annual Festival):

  • Date: The festival lasts for 10 days and usually takes place in the month of Kumbham (February-March).
  • Significance: The annual festival includes a series of rituals and processions. The highlight is the ‘Anayottam’ or elephant race, marking the beginning of the festivities.

Monthly Events at Guruvayur Temple

Guruvayur Temple hosts several important monthly events that are significant to the devotees. Here are the details:

Ekadasi:

  • Date: Ekadasi is observed on the 11th day of each lunar fortnight, both waxing (Shukla Paksha) and waning (Krishna Paksha) phases of the moon. This means it occurs twice a month.
  • Significance: Ekadasi is considered highly auspicious and is dedicated to Lord Vishnu. Devotees observe fasting and participate in special prayers and rituals at the temple.

Thiruvonam (Shravana Nakshatra):

  • Date: Thiruvonam Nakshatra (star) day is celebrated every month. The exact date varies according to the Malayalam calendar.
  • Significance: Thiruvonam is believed to be the birth star of Lord Vishnu. Special poojas and rituals are performed on this day, and it holds a significant place in the temple’s monthly calendar.

Pournami (Full Moon Day):

  • Date: Pournami is observed on the full moon day of every month.
  • Significance: The full moon day is considered very auspicious, and many devotees perform special prayers and rituals. The temple sees a larger number of visitors on this day.
