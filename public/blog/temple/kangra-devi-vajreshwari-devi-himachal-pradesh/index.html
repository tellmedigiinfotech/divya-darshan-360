Special Significance of the Bajreshwari Devi Temple

The Bajreshwari Devi Temple in Kangra holds significant spiritual importance, being one of the 51 Shakti Peethas. These are sacred sites where parts of Goddess Sati's body are said to have fallen. At this particular site, it is believed that the left breast of Sati fell, making it a potent center of feminine divine energy and worship. The temple itself is said to embody immense spiritual power and is a place of deep reverence for devotees.

Moreover, the temple has been a witness to numerous historical events and has been rebuilt several times, adding layers of historical and spiritual significance. Each reconstruction has been seen as a renewal of its spiritual strength and a testament to the enduring faith of its devotees.

Annual Event of the temple day and date?

The Bajreshwari Devi Temple in Kangra celebrates several annual events and festivals, with the most prominent being the Navratri festival. Navratri is a major event celebrated with great pomp and devotion at the temple. This festival spans nine nights (and ten days), usually occurring twice a year - once in the spring and once in the autumn. These periods correspond to the lunar months of Chaitra (March-April) and Ashvin (September-October).

During Navratri, the temple is elaborately decorated, and various rituals and cultural programs are held each day. Devotees from various parts come to participate in the festivities, seeking blessings from Goddess Bajreshwari.

Another significant event celebrated at the temple is Makar Sankranti, which usually falls on January 14th each year. This festival is marked by special rituals and offerings at the temple, attracting a large number of devotees.
