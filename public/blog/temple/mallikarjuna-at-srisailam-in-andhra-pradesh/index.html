Srisailam Devasthanam, a revered Hindu temple, is located in Srisailam, Andhra Pradesh.

Main Deity: The temple is primarily dedicated to Lord Shiva, known here as Mallikarjuna Swamy, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas.

Other Deities: Sub-deities within the complex include Bhramaramba Devi (a form of Parvati), which makes this site unique as it houses both a Jyotirlinga and a Shakti Peetha. Other auxiliary shrines and deities related to the Hindu pantheon also enrich the spiritual ambiance of this pilgrimage site.

This temple is a significant spiritual center, attracting devotees from across the country.

.Provide Construction/ scientific information about the temple

The Srisailam Temple, also known as the Sri Bhramaramba Mallikarjuna Swamy Temple, is a significant architectural and spiritual landmark in Andhra Pradesh. It is renowned for its historical, cultural, and architectural significance. Here's a detailed overview of the construction and scientific aspects of the temple:

Architectural Design and Construction
Historical Background

The Srisailam Temple complex dates back to ancient times and has been mentioned in various Hindu scriptures. It has been renovated and expanded by different dynasties over the centuries, including the Chalukyas, Kakatiyas, and Vijayanagara rulers.

Architectural Style

The temple complex is designed in the Dravidian architectural style, characterized by its grand gopurams (gateway towers), intricate carvings, and sprawling courtyards. The use of granite and other durable stones has ensured the temple's longevity.

Main Structures

Mallikarjuna Swamy Temple: The main sanctum houses the Jyotirlinga of Lord Shiva, known as Mallikarjuna Swamy. The sanctum is constructed in a classic Dravidian style with a pyramid-shaped vimana (tower) above it.

Bhramaramba Devi Temple: Adjacent to the main temple is the shrine of Goddess Bhramaramba, an incarnation of Parvati. The sanctum here is equally ornate, with detailed carvings and a smaller vimana.

Carvings and Sculptures

The temple walls and pillars are adorned with intricate carvings depicting scenes from Hindu mythology, floral motifs, and geometric patterns. These carvings showcase the high level of craftsmanship and artistry of the period.

Scientific Aspects
Seismic Resilience

The temple's construction incorporates traditional Indian architectural techniques that provide resilience against seismic activity. The interlocking stone construction without the use of mortar allows for flexibility and stability during earthquakes.

Natural Ventilation and Lighting

The temple design includes strategically placed windows and vents that allow for natural ventilation and lighting. This not only reduces the need for artificial lighting and cooling but also enhances the spiritual ambiance within the temple.

Geomagnetic Alignment

Like many ancient temples, the Srisailam Temple is believed to be built on a site with significant geomagnetic properties. The alignment of the temple structures is designed to harness these natural energies, which is thought to enhance the spiritual experience of the devotees.

Water Management

The temple complex historically incorporated advanced water management systems, including tanks and wells, to ensure a reliable water supply for rituals and the needs of pilgrims. The sacred Pathalaganga River, a tributary of the Krishna River, is considered holy and is used for various rituals.

Renovation and Preservation
Modern Preservation Techniques

Recent renovations have utilized modern preservation techniques to maintain the structural integrity and aesthetic beauty of the temple. This includes the use of chemical treatments to preserve the stone carvings and prevent weathering.

Infrastructure Development

Under various government schemes, including the PRASAD (Pilgrimage Rejuvenation and Spiritual Augmentation Drive) scheme, the temple complex has seen infrastructure improvements. This includes the development of amenities for pilgrims, improved access roads, and enhanced safety measures.

Cultural and Spiritual Significance
Spiritual Hub

The temple is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, making it a significant pilgrimage site for devotees of Lord Shiva. The unique combination of a Jyotirlinga and a Shakti Peetha (one of the 18 most important temples dedicated to the Goddess) adds to its spiritual significance.

Festivals and Rituals

The temple hosts numerous festivals and rituals throughout the year, drawing large crowds of devotees. Notable festivals include Maha Shivaratri, Navaratri, and Karthika Pournami, each celebrated with grandeur and devotion.

The Srisailam Temple stands as a testament to India's rich architectural heritage and spiritual tradition, blending ancient construction techniques with modern preservation efforts to maintain its significance for future generations.

Annual Event of the temple day and date

The annual event of Kasar Devi Temple is celebrated on the auspicious day of Kartik Poornima, which typically falls in the Hindu calendar month of Kartik (October-November) each year. The exact date may vary according to the lunar calendar. Therefore, it would be advisable to check with the temple authorities or their official website for the specific date of the annual event for the current year.

Monthly Event of the temple - day and date.

Kasar Devi Temple hosts a monthly event known as the "Kasar Devi Fair" or "Kasar Devi Mela." This fair usually takes place on the 13th day of each month according to the Hindu calendar. However, it's important to note that the date may vary slightly depending on the lunar calendar and other factors. Therefore, it's recommended to verify the exact date of the monthly fair with the temple authorities or their official website for the specific month you plan to attend.
