Construction and Scientific Information about Dakshineswar Kali Temple:

The Dakshineswar Kali Temple, constructed in 1855 by Rani Rashmoni, is an iconic example of Bengal temple architecture. The temple complex spans 25 acres and features a nine-spired (Navaratna) main temple dedicated to Goddess Kali. It also includes 12 smaller temples dedicated to Lord Shiva, arranged in two rows, and a temple dedicated to Radha-Krishna.

The temple’s architecture combines traditional Bengali design with elements of classical temple structures, ensuring durability and aesthetic appeal. The scientific approach to its construction ensures it withstands environmental and seismic stresses.

Annual Event of the temple day and date?

The major annual event at Dakshineswar Kali Temple is the Kali Puja, celebrated with grandeur typically in October or November during the new moon of the Hindu month Kartik. This festival involves elaborate rituals, decorations, and a large influx of devotees.

Monthly Event of the temple - day and date?

The monthly event at Dakshineswar Kali Temple is the Amavasya (New Moon) Pooja, conducted on the day of the new moon every month. This pooja includes special prayers and rituals dedicated to Goddess Kali.
