Provide Construction/ scientific information about the temple?

Construction
  • Location: Shravanabelagola, Karnataka, India
  • Main Attraction: 57-foot monolithic statue of Lord Gomateshwara (Bahubali)
  • Material: The statue is carved from a single block of granite.
  • Erected: Around 981 AD by Chamundaraya, a minister and commander of the Ganga Dynasty.
  • Architectural Style: Dravidian architecture with Jain influences.
Scientific Aspects
  • Monolith: The statue is one of the largest free-standing statues in the world.
  • Engineering: The precision of carving and stability of the statue demonstrates advanced engineering skills of the time.

The Gomateshwara Temple in Shravanabelagola holds immense spiritual and cultural significance:

  • Spiritual Symbolism: The 57-foot statue of Lord Gomateshwara (Bahubali) symbolizes peace, non-violence (ahimsa), renunciation, and self-control, which are core tenets of Jain philosophy.
  • Pilgrimage Site: It is a major pilgrimage site for Jains, attracting devotees from around the world, especially during the Mahamastakabhisheka, a grand anointing ceremony held once every 12 years.
  • Architectural Marvel: The monolithic statue, carved from a single block of granite, is a testament to the advanced engineering and artistic skills of the 10th century.
