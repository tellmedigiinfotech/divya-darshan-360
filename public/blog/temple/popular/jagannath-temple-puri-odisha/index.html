Condensed Information about the Place

Main Deity
  • Lord Jagannath: The principal deity of the temple, Lord Jagannath, is a form of Lord Vishnu. He is worshipped along with his siblings, Balabhadra (Balarama) and Subhadra.
Other Deities (Sub-deities)
  • Balabhadra: Also known as Balarama, he is the elder brother of Lord Jagannath and a prominent figure in the temple.
  • Subhadra: The sister of Lord Jagannath and Balabhadra, she is an essential part of the triad of deities worshipped in the temple.
  • Sudarsana: The symbolic representation of the wheel, which is often worshipped alongside the main deities.
  • Mahalakshmi: The goddess of wealth and consort of Lord Vishnu, she has a significant presence within the temple complex.
  • Saraswati: The goddess of knowledge, music, art, and learning.
  • Hanuman: The monkey god and devotee of Lord Rama, revered for his strength and loyalty.

Opening & Closing Timing of the Temple.

General Timing
  • Opening Time: 5:00 AM
  • Closing Time: 11:00 PM

The temple follows a strict daily schedule with various rituals and offerings conducted throughout the day. The main rituals include Mangala Aarti (early morning prayer), Sakala Dhupa (morning food offering), Madhyanha Dhupa (midday food offering), Sandhya Aarti (evening prayer), and Badasinghara Dhupa (late evening food offering).

Special Days like Festivals and Utsavas
  • Rath Yatra and Major Festivals: During major festivals such as Rath Yatra, Snana Purnima, and other significant utsavas, the temple may open earlier and close later than usual to accommodate the large influx of devotees and the extended rituals.
  • Special Festival Timing: Often, the temple may open around 4:00 AM and close around midnight, depending on the specific requirements of the festival and the associated rituals.

Days and Timings of Special Poojas

The Jagannath Temple in Puri offers several special poojas and rituals that take place throughout the day. Here are some of the key rituals:

1. Mangal Aarti
  • Time: Around 5:00 AM.
  • Description: This is the first ritual of the day, involving the waking up of the deities with prayers and offerings.
2. Abakash
  • Time: 6:00 AM.
  • Description: Morning rituals that include the cleaning and dressing of the deities.
3. Sakala Dhupa (Morning Food Offering)
  • Time: 8:00 AM to 9:00 AM.
  • Description: Offering of food (bhoga) to the deities.

4. Madhyanha Dhupa (Midday Food Offering):

  • Time: 11:00 AM to 1:00 PM.
  • Description: Main food offering of the day, involving elaborate preparations.
5. Sandhya Aarti (Evening Aarti)
  • Time: 7:00 PM to 8:00 PM.
  • Description: Evening prayers and offering of lamps to the deities.

6. Badasinghara Dhupa (Late Night Food Offering):

  • Time: 10:00 PM.
  • Description: The final food offering and ritual of the day before the deities are put to rest.

Temple Prashad services- Meals- procedure, payments if any, different class/lines? How to buy the ticket, if needed and timings?

Prashad (Mahaprasad) Services

The Jagannath Temple in Puri is famous for its Mahaprasad, which is considered highly sacred. The temple provides an organized system for distributing prashad to devotees.

Types of Prashad
1. Mahaprasad
  • Description: Mahaprasad refers to the cooked food offered to Lord Jagannath, which is later distributed to the devotees. It is available in both dry and wet forms.
  • Wet Mahaprasad: Includes items like rice, dal, vegetables, and sweets.
  • Dry Mahaprasad: Includes items like Khaja (a sweet made of flour, sugar, and ghee), and Nirmalya (dried rice offered to the deity).
2. Abhada
  • Description: Abhada refers to the temple’s community meal, served on banana leaves to devotees who sit together in the temple’s Anand Bazar area.
Procedure and Payment
1. Purchasing Mahaprasad
  • Procedure: Mahaprasad can be purchased from the Ananda Bazar inside the temple premises. Devotees can buy Mahaprasad either for personal consumption or to distribute among other devotees.
  • Payment: Payment is made at the counters in Ananda Bazar. Prices vary depending on the quantity and type of Mahaprasad. Generally, the costs are affordable to ensure accessibility to all devotees.
2. Booking Abhada Meal
  • Procedure: There are no advance booking procedures for Abhada meals. Devotees can join the meal by arriving at the Ananda Bazar during meal times.
  • Timings: Meals are served twice a day – midday (11:00 AM to 1:00 PM) and evening (7:00 PM to 9:00 PM). It is advisable to reach a bit early to find a spot.
Different Classes/Lines
  • General Queue: Most devotees join the general queue for Mahaprasad and Abhada meals.
  • Special Arrangements: During festivals and special occasions, the temple administration may organize special lines for senior citizens and physically challenged individuals to ensure they receive prashad comfortably.
Recommendations for Visitors
  • Arrive Early: To avoid long queues, it is recommended to arrive early, especially during festival days.
  • Follow Instructions: Adhere to the guidelines provided by the temple staff to ensure a smooth and respectful experience.

Is this temple registered under the state govt/District administration? Provide if the information is visible on the site.

Yes, the Jagannath Temple in Puri, Odisha is registered under the state government and district administration. The temple is managed by the Shree Jagannath Temple Administration (SJTA), which operates under the Shree Jagannath Temple Act, 1954. This act, passed by the Government of Odisha, ensures that the administration and governance of the temple and its endowments are vested in a committee called the Shree Jagannath Temple Managing Committee. This committee is a corporate body constituted by the state government, and its administrative structure has been further enhanced through amendments made in 2004.

The Chief Administrator of the temple is typically an IAS officer in the rank of Revenue Divisional Commissioner, and the temple administration is overseen by various officials, including the Collector of Puri and the Superintendent of Police, Puri​ (Shree Jagannatha Temple)​​ (Shree Jagannatha Temple)​​ (Puri Odisha Gov)​.

Annual Event of the temple day and date

Major Annual Events and Dates

1. Rath Yatra (Chariot Festival)
  • Date: Usually falls in June or July, depending on the Hindu lunar calendar (Ashadha month).
  • Significance: One of the most important festivals, where the deities Jagannath, Balabhadra, and Subhadra are taken out in grand chariots to the Gundicha Temple.
2. Snana Yatra
  • Date: Held on the full moon day of the Hindu month of Jyestha (May or June).
  • Significance: The deities are bathed with 108 pots of water from the Suna Kua (golden well), marking the ceremonial bath before the Rath Yatra.
3. Nabakalebara
  • Frequency: Occurs once every 12 to 19 years.
  • Significance: The idols of the deities are replaced with new ones made from sacred Neem wood. The last Nabakalebara was in 2015.
4. Makara Sankranti
  • Date: January 14th or 15th.
  • Significance: This festival marks the transition of the sun into the zodiac sign of Capricorn (Makara). Special rituals and offerings are made to the deities.
5. Chandan Yatra
  • Date: Begins on Akshaya Tritiya and lasts for 21 days (April or May).
  • Significance: The deities are taken to the Narendra tank and offered sandalwood paste to keep cool during the summer.
6. Deva Snana Purnima
  • Date: Full moon day of Jyestha month (May or June).
  • Significance: It is the bathing festival of Lord Jagannath, marking the beginning of the Rath Yatra preparations.
7. Bahuda Yatra
  • Date: Takes place nine days after the Rath Yatra.
  • Significance: The return journey of the deities from the Gundicha Temple back to the Jagannath Temple.
8. Suna Besha
  • Date: Day after the return from Bahuda Yatra (usually in July).
  • Significance: The deities are adorned with golden ornaments and displayed on their chariots.
