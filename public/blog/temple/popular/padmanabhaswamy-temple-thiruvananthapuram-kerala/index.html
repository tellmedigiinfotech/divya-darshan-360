Main Deity

  • Lord Padmanabhaswamy: The primary deity of the temple is Lord Padmanabhaswamy, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. The deity is depicted in the Anantha Shayana posture (eternal yogic sleep) on the serpent Anantha.

Other Deities (Sub-deities)

  • Narasimha Swamy: An incarnation of Vishnu depicted as part man, part lion.
  • Ganesha: The elephant-headed god of wisdom and remover of obstacles.
  • Ayyappa: A deity worshipped for his valor and wisdom.
  • Hanuman: The monkey god, revered for his devotion to Lord Rama and his immense strength.
  • Krishna: An incarnation of Vishnu, worshipped in various forms such as a child (Balakrishna) and as the charioteer of Arjuna in the Mahabharata.
  • Sastha: Another form of Ayyappa, worshipped for his protective and benevolent aspects.

C. Entry Restrictions for Foreigners

Does the temple provide accommodation facility to the pilgrims? If yes, what are the types of such facilities?What is the cost? How to book in advance?

Yes, the Padmanabhaswamy Temple offers accommodation facilities for pilgrims. The accommodation options include non-air-conditioned dormitories and air-conditioned waiting halls at the Sreepadam Resthouse. Here are the details:

1. Non-Air-Conditioned Dormitories
  • For 6 hours: ₹100 per person
  • For 12 hours: ₹200 per person

2. Air-Conditioned Waiting Halls (Sreepadam Waiting Hall):

  • 2 hours: ₹50 per person
  • 3 hours: ₹80 per person
  • 4 hours: ₹120 per person
  • 5 hours: ₹170 per person

The resthouse provides 60 beds and 28 chairs in the air-conditioned waiting area, and common restroom facilities are available for dormitory users. It's important to note that these facilities are exclusively available to Hindu devotees. Alcohol, smoking, and non-vegetarian food are not permitted on the premises.

For booking these accommodations, you can do so online, which is recommended especially during busy periods like weekends, public holidays, and festival days. Advance booking options are available to ensure your stay is secured before arrival.

Is this temple registered under the state govt/District administration? Provide if the information is visible on the site.

The Padmanabhaswamy Temple is not directly administered by the state government or district administration. Instead, the administration of the temple is managed by a trust headed by the Travancore Royal Family. This arrangement was upheld by the Supreme Court of India, which confirmed the royal family's rights to manage the temple affairs. The temple administration includes members nominated by the royal family and others designated by the state and central government, ensuring a collaborative oversight but with the royal family playing a central role.

For more detailed governance information, you might consider visiting the official temple or government websites related to the district of Thiruvananthapuram, where additional administrative details are likely provided.

Provide Construction/ scientific information about the temple

The Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple in Thiruvananthapuram is renowned not only for its spiritual significance but also for its architectural magnificence and historical importance. Here are some key aspects of its construction and architectural details:

The Vault: Below the temple are several vaults, which gained international attention when their treasures were discovered. These vaults, except for Vault B, have been opened and cataloged under the supervision of the Supreme Court of India, revealing a vast collection of gold, jewels, and other valuable artifacts.

The combination of its rich architectural design, historical treasures, and sacred importance makes the Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple a fascinating subject for both devotees and historians alike. For more detailed scholarly analysis and images that capture the detailed craftsmanship, visiting dedicated cultural heritage or architectural websites would provide deeper insights.
