Special Significance of Srikalahasti Temple

Srikalahasti Temple holds unique spiritual significance due to its Vayu Linga, representing the element of wind, one of the five Panchabhoota Sthalams. The temple is believed to have a powerful geomagnetic field, contributing to its mystical and healing properties. Devotees also believe that performing rituals like the Rahu-Ketu Pooja can alleviate astrological issues and bring positive changes in their lives.

Annual Event of Srikalahasti Temple

The main annual event celebrated at Srikalahasti Temple is the Maha Shivaratri festival, which typically occurs in February or March, depending on the Hindu lunar calendar. The exact date varies each year.

Monthly Event at Srikalahasti Temple

The temple holds various poojas and rituals throughout the month, but a notable monthly event is the Pradosham. Pradosham is observed twice a month on the 13th day of each lunar fortnight, dedicated to Lord Shiva, and is considered an auspicious time for worship and seeking blessings.
