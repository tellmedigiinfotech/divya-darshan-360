Construction and Scientific Information about Meenakshi Temple:

The Meenakshi Temple in Madurai, Tamil Nadu, is a historic architectural marvel built during the Nayak dynasty in the 16th century. The temple complex spans 14 acres and includes 12 towering gopurams (gateway towers) adorned with intricate carvings. The tallest tower, the southern tower, stands at 170 feet. The temple's design follows the Dravidian architectural style, characterized by its grandeur and attention to detail. The temple's layout is scientifically planned to align with ancient Vastu Shastra principles, ensuring harmonious energy flow.

Special Significance of Meenakshi Temple

The Meenakshi Temple in Madurai is renowned for its unique blend of religious, cultural, and architectural significance. It is dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi (Parvati) and Lord Sundareswarar (Shiva), symbolizing the divine couple. The temple is an important pilgrimage site in South India, known for its stunning Dravidian architecture and vibrant festivals, particularly the Meenakshi Thirukalyanam, which celebrates the divine marriage of Meenakshi and Sundareswarar.

Annual Event of the temple day and date?

Annual Event at Meenakshi Temple

The main annual event at the Meenakshi Temple is the Meenakshi Thirukalyanam (Divine Marriage of Meenakshi and Sundareswarar), which is a part of the Chithirai Festival. This grand celebration takes place in the Tamil month of Chithirai (April-May) and spans over ten days. The exact dates vary each year based on the Tamil calendar.

Monthly Event of the temple - day and date?

Monthly Events at Meenakshi Temple

The Meenakshi Temple hosts various monthly events and rituals, with the most prominent being the Pradosham, which occurs twice a month. Pradosham is observed on the 13th day of each lunar fortnight (Trayodashi).
