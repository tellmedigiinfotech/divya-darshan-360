Cultural and Spiritual Significance

  • The temple was built by Shreemant Dagdusheth Halwai, a wealthy sweet maker, and his wife, Lakshmibai, in memory of their son who died during a plague epidemic. The temple was constructed with the guidance of their spiritual guru, Shri Madhavnath Maharaj.
  • The temple has since become a symbol of faith, resilience, and community spirit in Pune.
2. Annual Festivals
  • The Ganesh Chaturthi festival is celebrated with grandeur at the temple, attracting millions of devotees. The celebrations include elaborate decorations, cultural programs, and community services.
  • The temple is also known for its participation in social causes, such as providing meals to the needy, running educational and healthcare programs, and supporting rural development.
3. Architectural Grandeur
  • The temple’s architecture is a blend of traditional and contemporary styles, with intricate carvings, a large prayer hall, and a stunning idol of Lord Ganesha adorned with gold and jewels.
  • The idol, crafted from a single marble block and decorated with gold and precious stones, symbolizes wealth, wisdom, and prosperity.
4. Community Services
  • The temple trust is deeply involved in various social welfare activities, including the provision of free meals, healthcare services, and educational programs. This involvement enhances the temple’s significance as a center for both spiritual and social upliftment.
Scientific and Environmental Aspects

Annual Event of the temple day and date?

The annual event at the Dagadushet Ganapati Temple centers around the Ganesh Chaturthi festival, which is celebrated with great fervor and devotion. This festival, dedicated to Lord Ganesha, typically occurs in the Hindu lunar month of Bhadrapada, falling around August or September each year.

Ganesh Chaturthi at Dagadushet Ganapati Temple

Event: Ganesh Chaturthi Celebration Typical Dates: Varies annually, generally between late August and early September. Duration: The festival lasts for 10 days.

Highlights of the Celebration
  • Installation of Ganesha Idols: The festivities begin with the installation of Lord Ganesha's idol in the temple and at various public places.
  • Daily Rituals and Pooja: Special rituals and poojas are performed daily, attracting thousands of devotees.
  • Cultural Events: The festival also includes a variety of cultural programs such as music concerts, dance performances, and spiritual discourses.
  • Visarjan Procession: The culmination of the festival is marked by the Visarjan (immersion) procession, where the idol of Lord Ganesha is carried through the streets accompanied by dancing, singing, and fanfare, before being immersed in water.
Visiting During Ganesh Chaturthi
  • Planning Your Visit: If you wish to experience the festival, it is advisable to plan your visit well in advance due to the large influx of devotees.
  • Accommodation: Booking accommodation near the temple ahead of time is recommended as places fill up quickly during the festival.

This annual event not only showcases the religious significance of the temple but also offers a deep dive into the spiritual and cultural richness of the local community.

Monthly Event of the temple - day and date?

The Dagadushet Ganapati Temple hosts a significant monthly event known as the Sankashti Chaturthi. This event is dedicated to Lord Ganesha and is celebrated every month on the fourth day after the full moon, according to the Hindu lunar calendar.

Sankashti Chaturthi at Dagadushet Ganapati Temple

Event: Sankashti Chaturthi Frequency: Monthly Date: 4th day after the full moon each lunar month

Highlights of Sankashti Chaturthi
  • Special Rituals: The day is marked by special rituals and prayers dedicated to Lord Ganesha. Devotees observe fasts and visit the temple to offer their prayers.
  • Moon Viewing: The rituals usually conclude in the evening after the moonrise, when devotees view the moon and perform the moon puja.
  • Prasad Distribution: Special prasad (sacred food offering) is distributed among the devotees after the evening rituals.
Planning Your Visit
  • Check the Lunar Calendar: The exact date of Sankashti Chaturthi varies each month, so it’s important to check a Hindu lunar calendar for the specific dates during your visit.
  • Evening Participation: Plan to attend the evening rituals to experience the full spiritual and cultural essence of the event.

This monthly event provides a wonderful opportunity to engage with the local devotional practices and experience the spiritual ambiance of the Dagadushet Ganapati Temple.
