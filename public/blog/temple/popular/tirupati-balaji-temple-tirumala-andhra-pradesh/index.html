Condensed Information about the Place

MAIN DEITY
  • Lord Venkateswara (Balaji): The main deity worshipped at the Tirupati Balaji Temple is Lord Venkateswara, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. He is revered as the "Kaliyuga Pratyaksha Daivam" (manifested deity of the Kali Yuga), believed to bestow prosperity and well-being upon his devotees.
OTHER DEITIES (Sub Deities)
  • Goddess Padmavathi: Located in Tiruchanur, close to Tirupati, this temple is dedicated to Goddess Padmavathi, the consort of Lord Venkateswara.
  • Lord Varaha Swamy: A shrine dedicated to Lord Varaha Swamy, an earlier incarnation of Lord Vishnu, is situated on the northern bank of the Swami Pushkarini lake.
  • Bedi Anjaneya Swamy: Situated opposite the main temple, this shrine is dedicated to Lord Hanuman (Anjaneya).
  • Vakulamatha Temple: This temple is dedicated to Vakulamatha, the mother of Lord Venkateswara.
  • Govindaraja Swamy Temple: Located in Tirupati, this temple is dedicated to Lord Govindaraja, a form of Lord Vishnu.

Opening & Closing Timing of the Temple.

General Timings
  • Opening Time: 2:30 AM
  • Closing Time: 1:30 AM (next day)

The temple is open for nearly 22 hours a day, allowing devotees ample time to have darshan (sight of the deity).

Daily Schedule
  • Suprabhatam (Early Morning Prayers): 3:00 AM - 3:30 AM
  • Darshan Timings: 3:30 AM - 1:30 AM (next day)
  • Ekantha Seva (Night Service): 1:30 AM
Special Days (Festivals and Utsavas)

During special occasions, festivals, and utsavas, the timings may vary to accommodate additional rituals and the influx of pilgrims. Some of the notable festivals include Brahmotsavam, Vaikunta Ekadasi, and other important celebrations.

Special Days Timings
  • Opening Time: As early as 2:00 AM
  • Closing Time: Extends till 2:00 AM (next day) or later, depending on the specific festival schedule.
Important Festivals and Their Extended Timings
1. Brahmotsavam
  • Opening Time: 2:00 AM
  • Closing Time: 2:00 AM (next day) or later
  • Special rituals and processions are conducted throughout the day and night.
2. Vaikunta Ekadasi
  • Opening Time: 12:00 AM (midnight) on the eve of Ekadasi
  • Closing Time: Late into the night of Ekadasi
  • Vaikunta Dwaram (Gate to Heaven) is opened, and special darshan arrangements are made.
3. Rathasapthami
  • Opening Time: 2:30 AM
  • Closing Time: 1:30 AM (next day)
  • A series of vahana sevas (processions) are held throughout the day.

Entry Fees and Privileges at Tirupati Balaji Temple

1. General Entry (Sarva Darshan)
  • Fee: Free
  • Privileges: Access to the general queue for darshan. The waiting time can be several hours, especially during peak seasons and festivals.
  • Restrictions: None for foreigners, except the standard rules applicable to all devotees, such as dress code and behavior.

2. Special Entry Darshan (Seeghra Darshan):

  • Fee: ₹300 per person
  • Privileges: Faster access to the deity through a special queue. This reduces waiting time significantly compared to the general entry.
  • Booking: Available online through the TTD website, TTD e-Darshan counters, and at the temple counters.
3. VIP Break Darshan
  • Fee: ₹500 to ₹10,000 (varies based on the type of VIP break darshan)
  • Privileges: Priority access for VIPs, longer view of the deity, and often includes special prasad.
  • Booking: Limited and requires a recommendation from a government official or through the TTD administration.
Additional Services and Fees
1. Arjitha Sevas (Paid Sevas)
  • Fee: Varies based on the type of seva (e.g., Suprabhatam Seva, Thomala Seva, Archana Seva).
  • Privileges: Participation in specific rituals and pujas performed in the temple. Devotees also receive special prasad and blessings.
  • Booking: Online through the TTD website, TTD information centers, or at the temple counters. These are highly sought after and need to be booked well in advance.
2. Prasadams
  • Laddu Prasadam: Can be purchased inside the temple premises.
  • Fee: ₹50 per laddu (approx.)
  • Other Prasadams: Various other prasadam options are available for purchase.
Entry Restrictions for Foreigners
  • No Specific Restrictions: Foreigners are welcome to visit the Tirupati Balaji Temple, but they must adhere to the general rules and regulations.
  • Dress Code: Modest attire is required. Men should wear dhotis or traditional attire, and women should wear sarees, salwar kameez, or traditional dresses.
  • Declaration: Foreigners are usually required to fill out a simple declaration form at the entry point, stating their religious intent and agreeing to abide by temple rules.
Special Fees and Declaration
  • Special Fees: Apart from the standard darshan and seva fees, there are no additional charges specifically for foreigners.
  • Declaration Form: Foreigners may be asked to fill out a declaration form confirming their faith and intent to participate in the rituals respectfully.

How to Reach Tirupati Balaji Temple - Tirumala, Andhra Pradesh

The Tirupati Balaji Temple, located in Tirumala, Andhra Pradesh, is accessible by various modes of transportation. Here are the details on how to reach the temple:

By Air
Tirupati Airport (Renigunta Airport)
  • Distance from Tirumala: Approximately 40 km
  • Connectivity: Regular flights from major Indian cities like Chennai, Hyderabad, Bangalore, Mumbai, and Delhi.
  • Airport Transfers: Taxis and buses are available from the airport to Tirupati town. From Tirupati, you can take a bus or taxi to Tirumala.
By Train
Tirupati Railway Station
  • Distance from Tirumala: Approximately 26 km
  • Connectivity: Well-connected with major cities across India, including Chennai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Mumbai, and Delhi.
  • Transfers to Tirumala: Buses and taxis are available from the railway station to Tirumala. The Andhra Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation (APSRTC) operates frequent bus services.
By Bus
Tirupati Bus Station
  • Distance from Tirumala: Approximately 23 km
  • Connectivity: Regular bus services from nearby cities and towns in Andhra Pradesh and neighboring states.
  • Direct Buses to Tirumala: APSRTC and private operators provide direct bus services to Tirumala from Tirupati Bus Station.
By Taxi
  • From Tirupati to Tirumala: Taxis are readily available for hire from Tirupati to Tirumala. The journey takes about 40 minutes to an hour.
By Car
  • Driving to Tirumala: Private cars and rented vehicles can drive up the hill to Tirumala. There are well-maintained ghat roads with scenic views, and ample parking facilities are available at Tirumala.
By Foot
Alipiri and Srivari Mettu Routes
  • Alipiri Metlu: A 9 km trekking path with 3,550 steps starting from Alipiri. It takes around 3-4 hours to reach the temple.
  • Srivari Mettu: A shorter 2.1 km route with 2,400 steps starting from Srinivasa Mangapuram. It takes around 1.5-2 hours to reach the temple.
Location Details

Address: Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD), Tirumala, Tirupati, Andhra Pradesh, India - 517504

Important Tips
  • Booking in Advance: It is advisable to book your travel tickets and accommodation in advance, especially during peak seasons and festivals.
  • Dress Code: Adhere to the temple’s dress code by wearing modest, traditional attire.
  • Local Transport: APSRTC operates regular bus services within Tirupati and to Tirumala, providing convenient and economical travel options.

For more detailed information on transportation and bookings, visit the official Tirupati Balaji Temple website or the TTD website.

Any specific Dress Code prescribed for entry to the place - men /women/for spl puja and festival days

The Tirupati Balaji Temple has a specific dress code for all devotees, which must be followed to enter the temple premises and participate in special poojas and festivals. This dress code ensures respect and reverence in the sacred space.

General Dress Code
For Men
  • Traditional Attire: Dhoti (white or off-white) with or without an angavastram (upper cloth) or kurta-pyjama.
  • Allowed: Shirt and pants (full length), though dhoti is preferred.
  • Not Allowed: Shorts, lungis, bermudas, T-shirts, or any Western casual wear.
For Women
  • Traditional Attire: Saree or half-saree with a blouse, or churidar with a dupatta.
  • Allowed: Salwar kameez with dupatta.
  • Not Allowed: Western outfits like jeans, skirts, T-shirts, or any casual attire.

Special Poojas and Festival Days Dress Code:

For Men
  • Mandatory Attire: Dhoti with an angavastram. Upper garments such as shirts are generally discouraged during special poojas and are sometimes required to be removed during the rituals.
  • Special Festivals: Wearing traditional silk dhoti and angavastram is highly encouraged.
For Women
  • Mandatory Attire: Saree or half-saree with a blouse. Women are expected to wear traditional Indian attire, especially during special poojas and festivals.
  • Special Festivals: Wearing traditional silk sarees is highly encouraged.
Important Notes
  • Head Covering: While not mandatory, it is considered respectful for men to cover their heads with an angavastram and for women to cover their heads with a dupatta or pallu of the saree when inside the temple premises.
  • Footwear: All devotees must remove their footwear before entering the temple premises.
Enforcement
  • The temple authorities strictly enforce the dress code, and entry may be denied if the prescribed attire is not adhered to.
  • For those who arrive without the appropriate attire, dhotis and sarees are often available for purchase or rent near the temple entrance.

The Tirupati Balaji Temple has specific rules regarding the entry of electronic gadgets into the temple premises to maintain the sanctity and security of the area.

Electronic Gadgets Policy

  • Not Allowed: Mobile phones are strictly prohibited inside the main temple premises.
  • Deposit Facility: Devotees can deposit their mobile phones at designated counters near the temple entrance. These counters are managed by temple authorities and provide safe storage.
2. Cameras
  • Not Allowed: Cameras and other photography or videography equipment are not allowed inside the temple premises.
  • Deposit Facility: Similar to mobile phones, cameras can be deposited at the designated counters near the temple entrance.
3. Laptops and Tablets
  • Not Allowed: Laptops, tablets, and other large electronic devices are prohibited within the temple premises.
  • Deposit Facility: These devices can be stored at the designated deposit counters.

Smart Watches and Small Electronic Gadgets:

  • Generally Not Allowed: Smart watches and small electronic gadgets that can record audio or video are not permitted inside the temple premises.
  • Deposit Facility: These can also be deposited at the designated counters.
Deposit Counters
Location
  • Near Temple Entrance: Several deposit counters are located near the main entrance to the temple complex. These counters are easily accessible and are well-marked for the convenience of devotees.
Process
  • Deposit Procedure: Devotees need to hand over their electronic gadgets at the deposit counters. They will receive a receipt/token which must be presented to retrieve their items after the visit.
  • Safety and Security: The deposit counters are secure and managed by temple authorities, ensuring the safety of the deposited items.
Operating Hours
  • Aligned with Temple Timings: The deposit counters are open according to the temple’s operating hours, from early morning until late at night.
Important Tips
  • Plan Ahead: Since electronic gadgets are not allowed, it is advisable to leave unnecessary gadgets at your place of stay.
  • Minimal Carry Items: Carry only essential items and avoid bringing any electronic devices to minimize the hassle of depositing and retrieving them.
  • Follow Guidelines: Adhere to the temple’s guidelines to ensure a smooth and respectful visit.

Provide Construction/ scientific information about the temple ?

The Tirupati Balaji Temple, also known as the Sri Venkateswara Swamy Temple, is a marvel of ancient Indian architecture and construction. Located in the hill town of Tirumala in Andhra Pradesh, it is one of the most revered and visited temples in India. Here are some detailed insights into its construction and scientific aspects:

Construction Details
1. Historical Background
  • Date of Construction: The temple's origins date back to ancient times, with various additions and renovations made by different dynasties over centuries. The main sanctum (Ananda Nilayam) is believed to have been constructed during the reign of the Pallava dynasty (9th century AD).
  • Patronage: The temple has received patronage from several South Indian dynasties including the Cholas, Pandyas, and Vijayanagara rulers, who contributed to its expansion and beautification.
2. Architectural Style
  • Dravidian Architecture: The temple is a classic example of Dravidian architecture, characterized by its intricate carvings, towering gopurams (gateway towers), and richly decorated sanctum sanctorum.
  • Materials Used: The primary materials used in the construction include granite and other local stones, along with gold plating on the dome of the sanctum.
3. Key Structures
  • Ananda Nilayam: The sanctum sanctorum where the main deity, Lord Venkateswara, resides. It features a gold-plated dome and intricate wooden and stone carvings.
  • Gopurams: The temple has several gopurams, with the main Rajagopuram being the tallest, standing at a height of 50 feet.
  • Mandapams: Various halls (mandapams) like the Ranga Mandapam and Tirumala Raya Mandapam are used for conducting rituals and accommodating pilgrims.
Scientific Aspects
1. Vastu Shastra
  • The temple's construction follows the principles of Vastu Shastra, an ancient Indian science of architecture that dictates the spatial geometry and orientation of buildings to harmonize with natural forces.
2. Geomagnetic Field
  • The temple is located on a unique geomagnetic equator, which is believed to enhance the spiritual vibrations and energy fields within the temple complex. This alignment is said to contribute to the profound spiritual experiences reported by pilgrims.
3. Acoustic Engineering
  • The design of the temple, especially the sanctum, is such that it amplifies the chants and prayers, creating a resonant and immersive spiritual atmosphere. The stone carvings and the placement of the sanctum optimize acoustic properties.
4. Gold Plating and Electroplating
  • The Ananda Nilayam and other parts of the temple are adorned with gold plating. The process involves intricate electroplating techniques to ensure the gold adheres well to the surfaces, maintaining its sheen and durability over centuries.

Annual Event of the temple day and date?

The Tirupati Balaji Temple hosts several significant annual events and festivals that attract millions of devotees from around the world. Here are some of the key annual events:

Brahmotsavam

  • Date: Generally held in September-October
  • Duration: 9 days
  • Description: The most important annual festival at the temple, Brahmotsavam includes grand processions, special rituals, and various cultural performances. The festival commemorates the consecration of the temple idol and is believed to be conducted by Lord Brahma himself.

Vaikunta Ekadasi

  • Date: Falls in December-January (exact date varies according to the Hindu lunar calendar)
  • Description: Vaikunta Ekadasi is one of the most auspicious days at the temple, marking the opening of the Vaikunta Dwaram (gate to heaven). It is believed that devotees who pass through this gate on this day attain salvation. Special prayers and rituals are conducted throughout the day.

Rathasapthami

  • Date: Generally falls in February (exact date varies according to the Hindu lunar calendar)
  • Description: Rathasapthami is a festival dedicated to the Sun God. On this day, Lord Venkateswara is taken on a procession around the temple on seven different vahanas (chariots), symbolizing the seven days of the week.

Ugadi

  • Date: Falls in March-April (exact date varies according to the Hindu lunar calendar)
  • Description: Ugadi marks the Telugu New Year and is celebrated with special prayers, rituals, and feasts at the temple. Devotees seek blessings for prosperity and happiness in the coming year.

Teppotsavam (Float Festival)

  • Date: Generally held in March
  • Duration: 5 days
  • Description: Teppotsavam involves the deities being taken out on a float in the Swami Pushkarini (temple tank). The festival is marked by elaborate decorations, special rituals, and a grand display of devotion.

Vasanthotsavam (Spring Festival)

  • Date: Generally held in April
  • Duration: 3 days
  • Description: Vasanthotsavam is a festival celebrating the arrival of spring. The deities are decorated with flowers, and special rituals and processions are conducted to mark the occasion.
