Provide Construction/ scientific information about the temple?

The Kanaka Durga Temple is located on the Indrakeeladri hill, which overlooks the Krishna River in Vijayawada, Andhra Pradesh. This temple is an example of traditional South Indian architecture, featuring intricate carvings and a towering gopuram (gateway tower). The construction integrates principles of Vastu Shastra, an ancient Indian science of architecture, to enhance spiritual energy. The temple’s design facilitates a serene environment, encouraging meditation and worship.

Annual Event of the temple day and date?

The Kanaka Durga Temple celebrates its major annual event during the festival of Dasara (Navaratri), which typically falls in September or October. This festival is celebrated with great devotion and includes special rituals, cultural programs, and a grand procession. The exact dates of the festival vary each year based on the lunar calendar.

Monthly Event of the temple - day and date?

The Kanaka Durga Temple holds a monthly event known as Pournami Garuda Vahana Seva on the full moon day of every month. This special ritual involves a procession of the deity on a Garuda Vahana (Eagle Vehicle) and includes various other religious ceremonies.
