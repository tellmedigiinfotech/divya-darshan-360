Main Deity

  • Surya (Sun God):
  • The main deity of the Konark Sun Temple is Surya, the Sun God. Surya is depicted riding a chariot driven by seven horses, symbolizing the seven days of the week and the seven colors of sunlight.
  • The temple showcases three images of Surya, placed to catch the rays of the sun at dawn, noon, and sunset, representing the sun’s daily journey.
Other Deities (Sub-deities)
  • Chhaya Devi:
  • The shadow goddess and consort of Surya. Often depicted alongside Surya in various artistic representations within the temple.
  • Mitra:
  • A deity associated with the sun and light, representing the friend aspect of Surya.
  • Aruna:
  • The charioteer of Surya’s chariot, symbolizing the dawn. Aruna is often depicted driving the chariot that carries Surya across the sky.
  • Ashwini Kumaras:
  • The twin deities associated with healing and medicine, depicted as the divine physicians who serve Surya.

These deities collectively contribute to the intricate and symbolic representation of the solar deity and his divine entourage, highlighting the temple’s emphasis on the celestial and cosmic significance of the Sun God.

Poojas and Rituals
1. Archana
  • Description: A ritualistic offering to the deity involving flowers, incense, and prayers to seek blessings.
  • Timings: Can be performed throughout the day during temple hours (6:00 AM to 8:00 PM).
  • Cost: The cost varies depending on the type and quantity of offerings. It is best to inquire at the temple for specific details.
2. Seva (Service)
  • Description: Special rituals and ceremonies conducted by temple priests on behalf of devotees for specific purposes like health, prosperity, or fulfillment of desires.
  • Timings: Similar to Archana, performed throughout the day.
  • Cost: Depends on the type of seva. Inquiries should be made directly at the temple.
3. Special Rituals for Couples
  • Description: A specific ritual for childless couples seeking blessings to conceive. Couples are required to perform pradakshina (circumambulation) around the temple both in the morning and evening for 21 days.
  • Timings: Morning and evening.
  • Cost: Inquire at the temple for specific details.

General Guidelines

The Sun Temple at Konark, being primarily a historical and architectural site, does not enforce a strict dress code like active religious temples. However, as a site of cultural and historical significance, visitors are expected to dress respectfully.

Recommended Attire
1. For Men
  • Preferred Attire: Casual yet modest clothing such as trousers, jeans, or long shorts paired with a t-shirt or shirt.
  • Avoid: Sleeveless shirts, extremely short shorts, or any attire considered too casual or revealing.
2. For Women
  • Preferred Attire: Comfortable and modest clothing such as long skirts, trousers, jeans, or traditional Indian wear like sarees and salwar kameez.
  • Avoid: Short skirts, shorts, tank tops, or any clothing that is too revealing or considered disrespectful in a cultural context.
Special Pooja and Festival Days
  • Special Dress Code: Although there is no formal dress code enforced during special poojas and festivals, it is recommended to wear traditional Indian attire to respect the cultural and religious atmosphere.
  • Men: Kurta-pajama or dhoti-kurta.
  • Women: Sarees, salwar kameez, or other traditional Indian dresses.
General Etiquette
  • Footwear: Comfortable walking shoes are recommended as visitors will spend a significant amount of time exploring the large temple complex.
  • Respect for the Site: As a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it is important to maintain decorum and show respect for the cultural and historical significance of the temple.

Is this temple registered under the state govt/District administration? Provide if the information is visible on the site?

The Sun Temple at Konark, also known as the Black Pagoda, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is managed by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI). This ensures its preservation and maintenance as a significant cultural and historical monument.

Administration and Governance
  • Archaeological Survey of India (ASI): The temple is under the administration of the ASI, which oversees its conservation and protection. This organization is responsible for maintaining the structural integrity and promoting the historical significance of the temple.
  • State Government: The Odisha state government, along with the district administration of Puri, plays a role in facilitating tourism and ensuring the temple's accessibility and safety for visitors.
Official Recognition
  • The temple is recognized as a key historical and cultural site by both national and international bodies. It was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1984, underscoring its global importance and historical value.

Provide Construction/ scientific information about the temple?

Architectural Design
1. Overall Structure
  • The Sun Temple is designed in the form of a colossal chariot mounted on 24 wheels, each about 12 feet in diameter, and drawn by seven horses. This structure symbolizes the Sun God's chariot as it moves across the sky.
2. Materials Used
  • The temple is primarily built from Khondalite rocks. The choice of this material is significant due to its durability and resistance to the saline and humid coastal climate of Konark.
3. Dimensions
  • The main temple (vimana) originally stood at around 229 feet tall, making it one of the tallest temples of its time. Although the main sanctum is no longer intact, the Jagamohana (audience hall) still stands at around 128 feet.
Carvings and Sculptures
1. Intricate Carvings
  • The walls of the temple are adorned with intricate carvings depicting various aspects of life, including deities, celestial beings, dancers, musicians, animals, and mythological scenes.
  • The carvings also include erotic sculptures, which are believed to represent the tantric traditions and the concept of mithuna (divine union).
2. Astronomical Significance
  • The 24 wheels of the chariot are not only decorative but also serve as sundials, capable of accurately telling the time of day. Each wheel has eight spokes, which can be used to calculate precise time.
  • The temple is aligned along the east-west axis, with the main entrance facing east, allowing the first rays of the sunrise to illuminate the deity inside the sanctum.
Engineering Marvels
1. Structural Integrity
  • The temple’s foundation is believed to be built on a bed of iron and magnetite, which might have contributed to its stability. There are legends suggesting that a massive lodestone was placed at the top of the temple, aiding in the temple's structural balance.
2. Ventilation and Illumination
  • The temple’s design includes strategic placement of windows and openings, ensuring proper ventilation and allowing natural light to illuminate the interior, enhancing the spiritual atmosphere.
Scientific Studies
1. Conservation Efforts
  • The Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) conducts ongoing conservation efforts to preserve the temple's structure and intricate carvings. Modern techniques are used to address issues like weathering, erosion, and biological growth on the stone surfaces.
2. Geological and Environmental Studies
  • Studies on the temple's material composition and the local environment help in understanding the long-term effects of the coastal climate on the structure. These studies inform conservation strategies to protect the temple from further deterioration.

Konark Dance Festival

The Konark Dance Festival is the major annual event held at the Sun Temple in Konark, Odisha. This grand celebration of classical dance forms takes place every year in the backdrop of the magnificent Sun Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The festival typically features performances by renowned dancers from across India, showcasing a diverse range of classical dance styles such as Odissi, Bharatanatyam, Kathak, Kuchipudi, and Manipuri.

Dates:Typical Dates: The festival usually spans five days in the first week of December, from December 1st to December 5th.
