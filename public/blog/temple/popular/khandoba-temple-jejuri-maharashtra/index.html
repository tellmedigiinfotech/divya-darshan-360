Provide Construction/ scientific information about the temple?

Architecture
  • The Khandoba Temple is situated on a hill, which requires a climb of about 200 steps to reach the main shrine.
  • The temple features traditional Maharashtrian architectural elements, with intricate carvings and sculptures adorning its walls.
  • The main sanctum houses the deity Lord Khandoba, who is a manifestation of Lord Shiva, and his consorts, Mhalsa and Banai.
  • The temple complex includes several smaller shrines and mandaps (halls), creating a serene and spiritually enriching environment.
Materials Used
  • The temple is constructed primarily from locally sourced stone and wood, with the use of lime plaster for the walls.
  • The stone steps and pathways leading up to the temple are well-maintained and designed to handle the large influx of devotees.
Scientific and Cultural Significance
  • The elevated location of the temple provides a panoramic view of the surrounding landscape, which adds to the spiritual experience of the visitors.
  • The hilltop location also ensures good air circulation, maintaining a pleasant climate within the temple premises.
  • The architectural design of the temple facilitates natural light and ventilation, enhancing the comfort of the devotees.
  • The temple is a significant cultural landmark, hosting major festivals like Champa Shashti and attracting thousands of devotees, which contributes to the local economy and cultural heritage.

Annual Event of the temple day and date?

The Khandoba Temple in Jejuri celebrates several important events, but the most significant annual event is the Champa Shashti Festival.

Champa Shashti Festival
  • Day and Date: Champa Shashti is celebrated on the sixth day of the bright fortnight of the Hindu month of Margashirsha (usually falls in November or December).
  • Significance: This festival marks the victory of Lord Khandoba over the demons Mani and Malla and is celebrated with great fervor. Devotees gather in large numbers to participate in rituals, processions, and the famous Bhandara (turmeric) ritual.
  • Duration: The festival lasts for six days, with the main day being the sixth day, Shashti.

Monthly Event of the temple - day and date?

The Khandoba Temple in Jejuri observes a significant monthly event known as Somvati Amavasya.

Somvati Amavasya
  • Day and Date: Somvati Amavasya occurs on a new moon day (Amavasya) that falls on a Monday (Somvar) according to the Hindu lunar calendar. This event happens several times a year, not necessarily monthly, depending on the alignment of the lunar calendar.
  • Significance: Devotees believe that worshipping Lord Khandoba on Somvati Amavasya brings special blessings and is highly auspicious. The temple sees a higher number of visitors on this day.
  • Rituals: Special poojas and rituals are performed, and devotees often observe fasts and partake in the temple activities.
