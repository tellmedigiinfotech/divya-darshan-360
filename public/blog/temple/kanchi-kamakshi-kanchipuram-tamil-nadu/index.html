A. Main Deity

Deity Name: Goddess Kamakshi

  • Description: Goddess Kamakshi is an incarnation of Parvati, the divine consort of Lord Shiva. She is worshipped as the supreme goddess of love and grace. The main idol of Kamakshi is seated in a Padmasana (a yogic posture) and holds a sugarcane bow, lotus, parrot, and a goad, symbolizing her control over the mind and her divine love.
B. Other Deities (Sub Deities)

Lord Varahi:

  • Description: Varahi is one of the Matrikas (a group of seven mother goddesses) and is considered the shakti (power) of Varaha, an avatar of Lord Vishnu.

Ayyappa Swamy:

  • Description: Lord Ayyappa is worshipped in this temple, signifying the harmonious blend of Saivism and Vaishnavism.

Adi Shankaracharya:

  • Description: A shrine dedicated to Adi Shankaracharya, the revered 8th-century philosopher who is credited with establishing the Kamakshi Temple in its current form.

Kalli Amman:

  • Description: Kalli Amman is another form of the goddess Durga, symbolizing power and protection.

Bangaru Kamakshi:

  • Description: Bangaru Kamakshi is a golden idol of Goddess Kamakshi, signifying her auspicious and benevolent aspects.

General Dress Code

Men:

  • Preferred Attire: Traditional attire such as dhoti-kurta or veshti (traditional South Indian attire) and angavastram (upper cloth).
  • Restrictions: Avoid wearing shorts, sleeveless shirts, and ripped jeans. Western casual wear is generally discouraged.

Women:

  • Preferred Attire: Traditional Indian attire such as sarees, salwar kameez, or long skirts with tops.
  • Restrictions: Avoid wearing short skirts, shorts, or revealing tops. Modest clothing is encouraged.

Traditional Attire:

  • Men: Dhoti-kurta or veshti and angavastram. Wearing traditional silk dhotis on special occasions is encouraged.
  • Women: Sarees or traditional South Indian attire. For special pujas, wearing traditional silk sarees is often encouraged.
  • Children: Boys may wear traditional attire like dhoti, and girls may wear pavada or lehenga.

Special Considerations:

  • Clean and Respectful Clothing: Ensure the attire is clean and respectful. This reflects reverence for the deity and the sanctity of the temple.
  • Head Covering: While not mandatory, it is appreciated if women cover their heads with a dupatta or scarf during special rituals and pujas.
Additional Guidelines

Footwear:

  • No footwear is allowed inside the temple premises. There are designated areas outside the temple to leave shoes and sandals.

Head Covering:

  • While not mandatory, women are generally encouraged to cover their heads with a dupatta or scarf, especially during special pujas and ceremonies.

Avoiding Leather Items:

  • Devotees are generally advised to avoid carrying or wearing leather items such as belts, wallets, or bags inside the temple premises.
Tips for Visitors
  • Respect Local Customs: Adhering to the dress code is a sign of respect for the local customs and the sanctity of the temple.
  • Prepare in Advance: If you are unsure about the dress code, carrying a shawl or scarf can be helpful.
  • Comfortable Clothing: Choose comfortable yet modest clothing suitable for the weather and the temple environment.
