Provide Construction/ scientific information about the temple?

The construction of Gurudwara Sri Hemkunt Sahib, located at an altitude of about 4,633 meters (15,197 feet) in the Himalayas, follows traditional Sikh architectural styles. The temple is designed with a star-shaped structure made of concrete, with a unique pentagonal shape that stands out amidst the snowy peaks. The interior houses the main prayer hall where the Guru Granth Sahib is placed. The site also includes lodging facilities and a Langar hall for pilgrims.

Gurudwara Sri Hemkunt Sahib holds significant spiritual importance in Sikhism. It is believed to be the place where Guru Gobind Singh, the tenth Sikh Guru, meditated in his previous life. The location is mentioned in the Guru's autobiographical work, "Bachitra Natak." Hemkunt Sahib is also renowned for its breathtaking natural beauty, situated amidst snow-capped mountains and a glacial lake, making it a revered pilgrimage site for Sikhs.
