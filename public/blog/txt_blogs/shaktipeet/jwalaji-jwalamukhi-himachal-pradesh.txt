Provide Construction/  scientific  information about the  temple?
The Jwalamukhi Temple is a unique structure known for its natural gas flames that emerge from fissures in the ground. The temple's architecture includes a dome and a golden spire, with the sanctum sanctorum housing the eternal flames instead of a traditional idol. These flames are scientifically attributed to natural gas emissions, which have been burning continuously for centuries.

Annual Event at Jwalamukhi Temple
Temple Festival: The main annual event at the Jwalamukhi Temple is the Navaratri festival, celebrated twice a year:
Chaitra Navaratri: March-April
Ashwin Navaratri: September-October
During these festivals, the temple hosts special poojas, rituals, and fairs, attracting thousands of devotees.
