A. Main Deity:
* Deity Name: Goddess Kamakshi
* Description: Goddess Kamakshi is an incarnation of Parvati, the divine consort of Lord Shiva. She is worshipped as the supreme goddess of love and grace. The main idol of Kamakshi is seated in a Padmasana (a yogic posture) and holds a sugarcane bow, lotus, parrot, and a goad, symbolizing her control over the mind and her divine love.
B. Other Deities (Sub Deities):
1. Lord Varahi:
   * Description: Varahi is one of the Matrikas (a group of seven mother goddesses) and is considered the shakti (power) of Varaha, an avatar of Lord Vishnu.
2. Ayyappa Swamy:
   * Description: Lord Ayyappa is worshipped in this temple, signifying the harmonious blend of Saivism and Vaishnavism.
3. Adi Shankaracharya:
   * Description: A shrine dedicated to Adi Shankaracharya, the revered 8th-century philosopher who is credited with establishing the Kamakshi Temple in its current form.
4. Kalli Amman:
   * Description: Kalli Amman is another form of the goddess Durga, symbolizing power and protection.
5. Bangaru Kamakshi:
   * Description: Bangaru Kamakshi is a golden idol of Goddess Kamakshi, signifying her auspicious and benevolent aspects.
