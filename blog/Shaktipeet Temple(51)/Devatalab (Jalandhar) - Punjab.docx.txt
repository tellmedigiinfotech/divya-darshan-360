	The Devi Talab Mandir, located in Jalandhar, Punjab, is a prominent Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Durga. It's one of the 51 Shakti Peethas, sacred places of worship where parts of Goddess Sati's body are believed to have fallen. The temple is especially known for housing the shrine of Tripurmalini, where it is said that the right breast of Sati fell. This makes it an important pilgrimage site for devotees.
Key Features of Devi Talab Mandir:
* Main Deity: The temple primarily worships Goddess Durga in her form as Tripurmalini.
* Historical Significance: The site is over 200 years old and features a blend of ancient and modern architectural styles.
* Special Attractions: The temple complex includes a replica of the Amarnath Cave, adding to its spiritual and cultural significance.
* Cultural Events: The temple is famous for hosting the annual "Harballabh Sangeet Sammelan," a major classical music festival attracting artists from across India.
* Facilities: It includes a charitable hospital and a sacred pond that are integral parts of the temple’s offerings to the community.

28. Annual Event of the temple day and date?
Devatalab Temple in Jalandhar hosts several significant annual events, with the most notable being the Harballabh Sangeet Sammelan. This classical music festival is one of the oldest and most prestigious music festivals in India, attracting musicians and devotees from across the country.
Key Annual Event:
* Harballabh Sangeet Sammelan:
   * Date: The festival is held annually from December 27th to December 30th.
   * Significance: It celebrates classical Indian music, featuring performances by renowned vocalists and instrumentalists. The festival is dedicated to Swami Harballabh, a revered saint and musician.
   * Activities: The event includes recitals of classical ragas, bhajans, and instrumental performances, creating a spiritually uplifting atmosphere.
Other Notable Festivals:
* Navratri: Celebrated twice a year, during Chaitra (March-April) and Sharad (September-October), Navratri is a major festival involving nine nights of worship, dance, and music dedicated to Goddess Durga.
* Durga Puja: Typically observed in September or October, this festival marks the victory of Goddess Durga over the buffalo demon Mahishasura. It involves elaborate rituals and cultural performances.
* Maha Shivaratri: Celebrated in February or March, this festival is dedicated to Lord Shiva and involves night-long vigils, prayers, and offerings.
