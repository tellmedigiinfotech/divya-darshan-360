27.  Provide Construction/  scientific  information about the  temple 
The Chintamani Temple in Theur is a significant historical and architectural marvel, reflecting the rich cultural heritage of Maharashtra. Here are some details about its construction and scientific aspects:
Construction Details:
1. Historical Background:
   * The temple was constructed in the 18th century under the patronage of the Peshwas, specifically by Madhavrao Peshwa. It showcases the architectural style and craftsmanship prevalent during the Maratha Empire​ (Wikipedia)​​ (Templeorigins.com)​.
   * The temple is dedicated to Lord Ganesha, who is worshipped as Chintamani Ganpati. The name "Chintamani" refers to a wish-fulfilling gem, symbolizing the deity's power to grant the desires of his devotees​ (Ganpati Sevak)​.
2. Architectural Features:
   * Temple Complex: The temple complex includes a main shrine dedicated to Lord Ganesha, surrounded by smaller shrines for other deities such as Lord Shiva (Mahadeva), Vishnu-Lakshmi, and Hanuman. The complex also features a Peshwa Wada (Peshwa Palace), which was the residence of Madhavrao Peshwa and now serves administrative purposes​ (Wikipedia)​​ (Heritage Temples)​.
   * Main Entrance and Mandapa: The main gate of the temple is located on the north side. The wooden mandapa (assembly hall) and stone sabha mandapa (assembly hall) are notable for their intricate carvings and architectural beauty. A black stone water fountain is present within the wooden mandapa​ (Heritage Temples)​.
   * Shikhara (Temple Tower): The temple tower or shikhara is restored with bricks and features a relatively plain exterior. The shikhara adds to the temple's grandeur and can be seen from a distance, guiding devotees to the temple complex​ (Heritage Temples)​.
   * Idol of Lord Ganesha: The idol in the garbhagriha (sanctum sanctorum) is self-manifested (svayambhu) and features a left-turned trunk. The eyes of the idol are studded with red beads and diamonds, adding to its divine appearance​ (Heritage Temples)​.
Scientific and Cultural Significance:
1. Self-Manifested Idol: The svayambhu idol of Lord Ganesha is considered to have emerged naturally, which holds significant spiritual and cultural importance for devotees. The concept of self-manifested idols is deeply rooted in Hindu beliefs and is associated with the divine presence and power of the deity​ (Heritage Temples)​.
2. Geomagnetic Fields and Spiritual Energy: While there is no specific scientific evidence of geomagnetic fields or cosmic energy like those associated with sites such as Kasar Devi, the spiritual ambiance and positive energy experienced by devotees at the Chintamani Temple are often attributed to its religious significance and historical legacy​ (Wikipedia)​.
Summary:
The Chintamani Temple in Theur is a blend of historical significance, architectural beauty, and spiritual importance. Constructed in the 18th century under the Maratha Empire's patronage, it features intricate carvings, a self-manifested idol, and a complex that includes multiple shrines and a Peshwa Palace. While primarily known for its religious and cultural heritage, the temple's serene environment and spiritual energy continue to attract devotees and visitors from around the world.

   * Timing: Celebrated from the first to the seventh day of the Hindu month of Bhadrapada, with the main day being Ganesh Chaturthi on the fourth day.
   * Details: This festival marks the birth of Lord Ganesha and includes various rituals, cultural programs, and a fair held in the temple premises.
2. Ganesha Jayanti (Magha):
   * Timing: Celebrated from the first to the eighth day of the Hindu month of Magha, with the main event on Ganesha Jayanti, which falls on the fourth day.
   * Details: This festival commemorates the birthday of Lord Ganesha and includes special poojas, processions, and community activities.
3. Rama-Madhav Punyotsav (Kartik):
   * Timing: Celebrated on the eighth day of the Hindu month of Kartik.
   * Details: This event commemorates the death anniversary of Madhavrao Peshwa and his wife Ramabai. The temple organizes a fair and special rituals during this time​ (Wikipedia)​​ (Myoksha Travels)​​ (RVA Temples)​​ (All World Temple)​.

32.   Monthly Event of the temple - day and date.
Monthly Events at Chintamani Temple, Theur
The Chintamani Temple in Theur observes special rituals and celebrations on a monthly basis, particularly on Angaraki Sankashti Chaturthi. This event is celebrated on the fourth day of the waning phase of the moon when it falls on a Tuesday. Here are the details:
1. Angaraki Sankashti Chaturthi:
   * Significance: This day is considered highly auspicious for worshipping Lord Ganesha. Devotees observe fasting and participate in special rituals to seek the removal of obstacles and receive blessings.
   * Timings: The temple opens early at around 4:00 AM and closes late around 11:00 PM to accommodate the large number of devotees who come for darshan.
   * Special Poojas: Special poojas and aartis are performed throughout the day. The Maha Aarti, a significant ritual, usually takes place around 7:30 AM.
2. Darshan Timings on Special Days:
   * Morning Darshan: 4:00 AM to 1:00 PM
   * Evening Darshan: 2:00 PM to 11:00 PM

Regular Monthly Rituals:
* Madhyan Aarti: Held at 12:00 PM.
* Shej Aarti: Concludes the day’s rituals at 10:00 PM.
These monthly events provide devotees with an opportunity to engage in spiritual activities and seek the blessings of Lord Ganesha in a more focused and celebratory environment.
