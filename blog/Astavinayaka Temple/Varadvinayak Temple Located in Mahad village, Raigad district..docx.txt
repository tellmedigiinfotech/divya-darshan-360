24. Provide Construction/scientific information about the temple.
The Varadvinayak Temple, built in 1725 by Peshwa General Ramji Mahadev Biwalkar, features traditional Maharashtrian architecture. The temple has a simple design with a tiled roof and a dome that stands 25 feet high. Unique features include the Nandadeep (an eternal lamp) that has been burning since 1892 and a Gomukh (cow's face) spout from which holy water flows. The idol of Lord Ganesha, found in 1690, is self-manifested and faces east with a left-turned trunk. The temple premises also house idols of Mushika, Navagraha, and Shivalinga.

Annual Events
The Varadvinayak Temple in Mahad celebrates several major annual festivals:
a. Ganesh Chaturthi:
   i. Timing: Celebrated from the first day (Pratipada) to the fifth day (Panchami) of Bhadrapada Shukla (waxing phase of the moon), which usually falls in August-September.
   ii. Details: This festival marks the birth of Lord Ganesha and includes elaborate rituals, cultural performances, and a grand fair.
b. Maghi Utsav:
   i. Timing: Celebrated from the first day (Pratipada) to the sixth day (Shashthi) of Magha Shukla (waxing phase of the moon), which usually falls in January-February.
   ii. Details: This festival includes special rituals and prayers. It is believed that consuming a coconut received as prasad during this festival blesses the devotee with a son.
