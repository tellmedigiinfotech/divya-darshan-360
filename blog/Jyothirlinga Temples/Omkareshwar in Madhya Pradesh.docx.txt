A.MAIN DEITY- B. OTHER DEITIES(Sub deities)-port


At the Omkareshwar Temple in Madhya Pradesh, the main deity worshipped is Lord Shiva, represented as Omkareshwar Jyotirlinga, one of the twelve sacred Jyotirlingas in Hinduism. The temple is located on an island shaped like the sacred Hindu symbol "Om" in the Narmada River, which adds to its spiritual significance. 

Additionally, there are several sub-deities also venerated within the temple complex. Notably, the temple honors Mamleshwar (Amaleshwar) on the mainland, considered another manifestation of Lord Shiva. This temple showcases the intertwined nature of Omkareshwar and Mamleshwar, making it a unique pilgrimage site. There are also shrines for other deities such as Parvati, Ganesha, and Nandi within the complex, enriching the spiritual tapestry of this sacred site.

      24. .Provide Construction/  scientific  information about the  temple .
        The Omkareshwar Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is renowned for its architectural and spiritual significance. This temple is one of the twelve Jyotirlinga shrines located on Mandhata island in the Narmada River, Madhya Pradesh. The island's shape, resembling the Hindu Om symbol, adds to the sanctity and unique nature of this location. 

The temple's construction showcases the Nagara style of architecture, notable for its high spires and intricate stone carvings. Historical records are not precise on the original date of construction, but the temple structure as it stands today includes contributions from various periods. Notably, King Udayaditya in 1063 and Raja Bharat Singh Chauhan in 1195 have been significant in its historical renovations and expansions. 

The Omkareshwar Temple has undergone several renovations throughout history, reflecting the devotion and reverence it commands. It was also significantly restored by Rani Ahalya Bai Holkar in the 19th century, ensuring the preservation of its ancient heritage and religious importance.

Annual Event of Omkareshwar Temple 
The main annual event at Omkareshwar Temple is Mahashivratri, a major Hindu festival dedicated to Lord Shiva. This festival typically falls on the 14th day of the dark fortnight in the month of Phalguna, which corresponds to February or March in the Gregorian calendar. 

Mahashivratri is celebrated with great fervor and devotion. It includes various rituals and special pujas conducted throughout the day and night. Devotees from all over the country visit the temple to participate in the celebrations and seek blessings from Lord Shiva.
