The Guruvayur Temple, dedicated to Lord Krishna, is one of the most important and ancient temples in Kerala. Here's a detailed look at the construction and some scientific aspects of the temple:
Construction Details
1. Architecture: The temple follows the typical Kerala style of architecture, characterized by sloping roofs, wooden structures, and intricate carvings. The sanctum sanctorum (Sreekovil) is made of granite and is square-shaped with a pyramidal roof covered with copper plates.
2. Main Structures:
   * Sreekovil: The sanctum sanctorum where the idol of Lord Krishna is placed. It is built in traditional Kerala style with a conical roof.
   * Nalambalam: The inner courtyard that surrounds the sanctum sanctorum.
   * Balikkalpura: The altar for offerings.
   * Koothambalam: The hall for performing arts.
   * Deepastambham: The light tower, often covered with brass or gold plating.
   * Gopuram: The monumental tower at the entrance, decorated with sculptures.
3. Materials: The primary materials used in the construction include granite for the foundation and sanctum, laterite stones, wood, and metals like brass and copper for the roofing and cladding.
4. Idol: The main deity, Lord Krishna, is depicted as a child (Bala Krishna). The idol is made of a unique stone called Patala Anjana, believed to have medicinal properties and the ability to purify the surroundings.
Scientific Aspects
1. Vaastu Shastra: The temple’s design follows the principles of Vaastu Shastra, the traditional Indian system of architecture, which aligns structures with natural and cosmic energies to ensure harmony and positive vibrations.
2. Energy Flow: The sanctum sanctorum is strategically placed to be the core energy center of the temple. The construction and layout are designed to channel positive energy towards devotees during darshan (viewing of the deity).
3. Geographical Location: Guruvayur is located in a region that is believed to have a high concentration of magnetic and cosmic energy, enhancing the spiritual experience of the devotees.
4. Acoustics: The temple's architecture, including the Koothambalam (performing arts hall), is designed to optimize acoustics. This ensures that the chanting of hymns and temple music resonate clearly throughout the temple complex.
5. Environmental Harmony: The materials used in the construction, such as wood and natural stones, ensure that the temple remains cool and comfortable, even in the tropical climate of Kerala. The use of copper and brass in the roofing helps in maintaining a balanced thermal environment.
6. Scientific Healing: The idol of Lord Krishna is made of Patala Anjana Stone, which is known for its medicinal properties. Devotees believe that the very presence in the temple and sight of the idol can have healing effects.
Historical and Cultural Significance
1. Historical Records: The temple's history dates back over 5,000 years, with mentions in various ancient texts and legends. It is believed that the temple was established by the sage Parashurama.
2. Cultural Hub: Guruvayur Temple is not only a place of worship but also a cultural center where traditional art forms like Kathakali, Carnatic music, and other classical dances are regularly performed.

   * Date: This festival is observed on the Ekadasi (11th day) of the waxing moon in the Malayalam month of Vrischikam (November-December).
   * Significance: Guruvayur Ekadasi is considered the most auspicious day at the temple. It marks the day when Lord Krishna revealed the Bhagavad Gita to Arjuna. The day is filled with special rituals, offerings, and a grand procession.
2. Chembai Sangeetholsavam:
   * Date: This annual music festival starts on the 14th of November and lasts for about 11-15 days.
   * Significance: It is one of the largest Carnatic music festivals in India, held in honor of the late Chembai Vaidyanatha Bhagavatar. Musicians from all over the country participate in this grand event.
3. Ulsavam (Annual Festival):
   * Date: The festival lasts for 10 days and usually takes place in the month of Kumbham (February-March).
   * Significance: The annual festival includes a series of rituals and processions. The highlight is the ‘Anayottam’ or elephant race, marking the beginning of the festivities.

Monthly Events at Guruvayur Temple
Guruvayur Temple hosts several important monthly events that are significant to the devotees. Here are the details:
1. Ekadasi:
   * Date: Ekadasi is observed on the 11th day of each lunar fortnight, both waxing (Shukla Paksha) and waning (Krishna Paksha) phases of the moon. This means it occurs twice a month.
   * Significance: Ekadasi is considered highly auspicious and is dedicated to Lord Vishnu. Devotees observe fasting and participate in special prayers and rituals at the temple.
2. Thiruvonam (Shravana Nakshatra):
   * Date: Thiruvonam Nakshatra (star) day is celebrated every month. The exact date varies according to the Malayalam calendar.
   * Significance: Thiruvonam is believed to be the birth star of Lord Vishnu. Special poojas and rituals are performed on this day, and it holds a significant place in the temple’s monthly calendar.
3. Pournami (Full Moon Day):
   * Date: Pournami is observed on the full moon day of every month.
   * Significance: The full moon day is considered very auspicious, and many devotees perform special prayers and rituals. The temple sees a larger number of visitors on this day.
