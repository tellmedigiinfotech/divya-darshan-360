A. Main Deity:
* Surya (Sun God):
   * The main deity of the Konark Sun Temple is Surya, the Sun God. Surya is depicted riding a chariot driven by seven horses, symbolizing the seven days of the week and the seven colors of sunlight.
   * The temple showcases three images of Surya, placed to catch the rays of the sun at dawn, noon, and sunset, representing the sun’s daily journey.
B. Other Deities (Sub-deities):
* Chhaya Devi:
   * The shadow goddess and consort of Surya. Often depicted alongside Surya in various artistic representations within the temple.
* Mitra:
   * A deity associated with the sun and light, representing the friend aspect of Surya.
* Aruna:
   * The charioteer of Surya’s chariot, symbolizing the dawn. Aruna is often depicted driving the chariot that carries Surya across the sky.
* Ashwini Kumaras:
   * The twin deities associated with healing and medicine, depicted as the divine physicians who serve Surya.
These deities collectively contribute to the intricate and symbolic representation of the solar deity and his divine entourage, highlighting the temple’s emphasis on the celestial and cosmic significance of the Sun God.

Registration and Administration of Sun Temple, Konark
The Sun Temple at Konark, also known as the Black Pagoda, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is managed by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI). This ensures its preservation and maintenance as a significant cultural and historical monument.
Administration and Governance:
* Archaeological Survey of India (ASI): The temple is under the administration of the ASI, which oversees its conservation and protection. This organization is responsible for maintaining the structural integrity and promoting the historical significance of the temple.
* State Government: The Odisha state government, along with the district administration of Puri, plays a role in facilitating tourism and ensuring the temple's accessibility and safety for visitors.
Official Recognition:
* The temple is recognized as a key historical and cultural site by both national and international bodies. It was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1984, underscoring its global importance and historical value.

Architectural Design:
1. Overall Structure:
   * The Sun Temple is designed in the form of a colossal chariot mounted on 24 wheels, each about 12 feet in diameter, and drawn by seven horses. This structure symbolizes the Sun God's chariot as it moves across the sky.
2. Materials Used:
   * The temple is primarily built from Khondalite rocks. The choice of this material is significant due to its durability and resistance to the saline and humid coastal climate of Konark.
3. Dimensions:
   * The main temple (vimana) originally stood at around 229 feet tall, making it one of the tallest temples of its time. Although the main sanctum is no longer intact, the Jagamohana (audience hall) still stands at around 128 feet.
