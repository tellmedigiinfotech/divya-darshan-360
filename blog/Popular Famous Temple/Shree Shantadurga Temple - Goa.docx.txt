21. Is this temple registered under the state govt/District administration? Provide if the information is visible on the site?
The Padmanabhaswamy Temple in Thiruvananthapuram is not directly managed by the state government or district administration. Instead, it is overseen by the erstwhile royal family of Travancore. In a landmark decision, the Supreme Court of India upheld the hereditary rights of the Travancore royal family to manage the temple's affairs. This ruling overturned a previous Kerala High Court judgment which had directed the state government to take over the temple's management​ (Hindustan Times)​​ (Hindustan Times)​​ (Telegraph India)​.
An administrative committee manages the temple, with oversight by the Thiruvananthapuram district judge, ensuring proper administration while respecting the traditional custodianship of the royal family​ (Telegraph India)​.

Architectural Features
1. Dravidian Style Architecture:
   * The Padmanabhaswamy Temple is a classic example of Dravidian style architecture, characterized by its towering gopurams (gateway towers) and intricate carvings.
   * The main gopuram (Eastern Tower) stands at 100 feet tall and is a significant landmark in Thiruvananthapuram.
2. Sree Kovil (Sanctum Sanctorum):
   * The sanctum sanctorum houses the idol of Lord Vishnu in a reclining posture, which is 18 feet long and is made from a special mixture called "Katu-Sarkara-Yogam" (a combination of herbs and other materials).
   * The idol is an impressive amalgamation of three parts: the head and chest (Anantha), the mid-portion (Panchajanya), and the feet (Sudarsana).
3. Corridors and Mandapams:
   * The temple features long corridors with 365 and 1/4 sculpted granite stone pillars, each bearing elaborate carvings.
   * The Ottakal Mandapam (single stone platform) in front of the sanctum is made from a single slab of granite and serves as the site for important rituals.
4. Murals and Frescoes:
   * The walls of the temple are adorned with ancient murals and frescoes that depict scenes from Hindu mythology, particularly stories of Lord Vishnu.
Scientific and Engineering Aspects
1. Ventilation and Natural Lighting:
   * The temple's design incorporates natural ventilation and lighting through strategically placed windows and vents, maintaining a cool and serene environment inside the temple.
2. Granite and Mixture Materials:
   * The use of granite and a special mixture in constructing the idol ensures durability and longevity, showcasing ancient Indian expertise in material science.
3. Water Management System:
   * The temple complex includes a sacred tank (Padma Theertham) that plays a vital role in water management and purification rituals.
Historical and Cultural Significance
* Historical Records: The temple has a rich history documented in ancient texts, and its significance is mentioned in several Puranas.
* Royal Patronage: Historically, the temple has been under the patronage of the Travancore royal family, who played a crucial role in its upkeep and rituals.

28. Annual Event of the temple day and date?
The Padmanabhaswamy Temple in Thiruvananthapuram celebrates several significant annual events and festivals, each with its unique rituals and cultural importance. Here are some of the main festivals:
1. Painkuni Festival
* Timing: March/April
* Description: A ten-day festival starting with the hoisting of the ceremonial flag (kodiyettu). Huge fiber glass figures of the Pandavas are placed at the eastern entrance, and various special rituals are performed daily. The festival includes a royal hunt ritual (palli vetta) and ends with the aarattu procession to Shanghumugham Beach for the ritual immersion of idols​ (Kerala Tourism)​.
2. Alpashi Utsavam
* Timing: October/November
* Description: Another ten-day festival similar to the Painkuni, featuring the flag hoisting ceremony, processions with different vahanas (vehicles), and the ritual of Pallivetta. The festival concludes with the aarattu ceremony at Shanghumugham Beach​ (Myoksha Travels)​​ (Kerala Tourism)​.
3. Laksha Deepam
* Timing: Once every six years
* Description: A grand festival where one lakh (100,000) lamps are lit, decorating the temple premises. The holy Vedas are recited for 56 days before the festival day. This spectacular event attracts many devotees and visitors​ (Culture and Heritage)​.
4. Vishu
* Timing: April (Malayalam New Year)
* Description: Celebrated with fireworks, and the Vishukanni (a ritual arrangement of auspicious items) is set up inside the inner sanctum. It marks the start of the Malayalam New Year and is celebrated with great enthusiasm​ (Myoksha Travels)​.
5. Swargavathil Ekadasi
* Timing: December/January
* Description: Known as Vaikunta Ekadasi, it is believed that Lord Vishnu opens the gates of Vaikunta (heaven) on this day. Devotees observe fasts and engage in worship, seeking liberation from the cycle of birth and death​ (Myoksha Travels)​.
6. Onam
* Timing: August/September
* Description: Commemorates the Vamana avatar of Lord Vishnu and the homecoming of King Mahabali. The Onavillu Charthal (presentation of ceremonial bows) is a highlight of the festival​ (Myoksha Travels)​.
