Registration and Administration of Dwarkadhish Temple, Dwarka
The Dwarkadhish Temple, also known as the Jagat Mandir, is a significant religious site located in Dwarka, Gujarat. The temple is registered and managed under the supervision of the state government and the district administration.
Administration:
* Dwarka Mandir Vyavasthapan Samiti: The administration of the temple is handled by the Dwarka Mandir Vyavasthapan Samiti. This committee includes bureaucrats, representatives from local bodies, and residents.
* Historical Registration: The temple has been under government supervision since the early 20th century, initially managed by the Gaekwad state. It was later registered as a trust with the Charity Commissioner to ensure proper management and adherence to religious practices.
* Current Administration: The temple's administration involves members from various sectors, including local government officials and representatives from the Reliance Group. This collective ensures the smooth functioning and maintenance of the temple premises and related activities.

1. Historical Significance:
   * The Dwarkadhish Temple, also known as the Jagat Mandir, is believed to have been originally built by Vajranabh, the great-grandson of Lord Krishna, over 2,500 years ago. The current structure is a later reconstruction dating back to the 15th century, with significant additions made in the 16th and 17th centuries​ (Devbhumi Dwarka)​​ (India Travel Helper)​.
2. Structural Composition:
   * Materials: The temple is constructed primarily from limestone and sandstone. Limestone is chosen for its durability and resistance to the coastal weather conditions.
   * Design: The temple features a five-story structure supported by 72 pillars. It stands on a 60x60 meter platform and has a tall spire (shikhara) reaching approximately 78 meters (256 feet) high​ (Wikipedia)​​ (India Travel Helper)​.
3. Entrances:
   * Moksha Dwara (Door to Salvation): The main entrance used by pilgrims, leading to the primary market area.
   * Swarga Dwara (Gate to Heaven): The exit door that leads to the Gomti River, accessible via 56 steps​ (Wikipedia)​​ (India Travel Helper)​.
Scientific and Engineering Marvels:
1. Structural Integrity:
   * The use of limestone and sandstone ensures the temple’s longevity and resilience against the saline and humid conditions of the coastal environment. The materials have been meticulously chosen and arranged to withstand erosion and other weather-related challenges.
2. Acoustics and Ventilation:
   * The temple’s design incorporates excellent acoustics, which enhances the chanting and prayers conducted within the premises. The open design and strategic placement of windows and vents ensure natural ventilation, keeping the interior cool and comfortable for devotees​ (PNDwarka)​​ (India Travel Helper)​.
3. Flag Hoisting Ceremony:
   * The temple is known for its large flag, which is changed multiple times a day. The flag, measuring 52 yards in length, is a significant aspect of the temple's identity and is visible from several kilometers away. The flagpole and mechanism are designed to handle the frequent changes and the coastal winds effectively​ (Wikipedia)​.
4. Cultural and Religious Significance:
   * The temple is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites, making it a vital religious destination for Hindus. It is also one of the Sapta Puri, the seven holy cities of India, enhancing its spiritual importance​ (Wikipedia)​.
Preservation and Renovation:
1. Renovation Efforts:
   * The temple has undergone several renovations, the most significant being managed by the Dwarka Mandir Vyavasthapan Samiti with support from the government and private organizations like Reliance. These renovations ensure the preservation of the temple’s structural and aesthetic integrity​ (Devbhumi Dwarka)​​ (PNDwarka)​.
2. Scientific Studies:
   * Various archaeological and scientific studies have been conducted to understand the temple's historical layers and structural composition better. These studies help in planning effective conservation strategies.

Annual Events at Dwarkadhish Temple, Dwarka
Key Annual Festivals and Their Dates:
1. Janmashtami:
   * Date: Celebrated on the eighth day of the dark fortnight in the month of Bhadrapada (August-September).
   * Significance: This is the most important festival at the Dwarkadhish Temple, commemorating the birth of Lord Krishna. The temple is adorned with lights and decorations, and special rituals and cultural programs are conducted over several days, culminating in the midnight darshan of the idol of Lord Krishna amidst chanting and celebrations​ (Templeduniya)​​ (Gujarat Tourism)​.
2. Holi:
   * Date: Celebrated in March.
   * Significance: Known as the festival of colors, Holi at the Dwarkadhish Temple involves vibrant celebrations, symbolizing the playful nature of Krishna and Radha. Devotees throw colors at each other, sing bhajans, and immerse themselves in the joyful spirit of the festival​ (Templeduniya)​​ (Myoksha Travels)​.
3. Diwali:
   * Date: Celebrated in October-November.
   * Significance: The festival of lights, Diwali, illuminates the temple with diyas and rangoli designs. Special pujas are performed, and the temple premises glow with an ethereal light, creating a magical atmosphere​ (Templeduniya)​​ (Gujarat Tourism)​.
4. Rath Yatra:
   * Date: On the second day of the bright fortnight of Ashadha (June-July).
   * Significance: This chariot festival commemorates the journey of Lord Krishna and his brother Balrama. A grand procession with a decorated chariot carrying the idols of Krishna, Balarama, and Subhadra takes place through the streets of Dwarka​ (Templeduniya)​​ (Myoksha Travels)​.
5. Tulsi Vivah:
   * Date: Begins on the 11th day of the Kartik month (October-November) and continues for four days.
   * Significance: This festival symbolizes the ceremonial marriage of Lord Vishnu (in the form of Dwarkadhish) with Tulsi Devi. The temple is beautifully decorated, and special rituals are performed during this period​ (Myoksha Travels)​​ (Gujarat Tourism)​.
6. Sharad Purnima:
   * Date: Celebrated on the full moon night of the Ashwin month (September-October).
   * Significance: This festival marks a night of divine love and devotion, with rituals and prayers performed to seek blessings for happiness and prosperity​ (Templeduniya)​​ (Visit Dwarka)​.
29. Monthly Event of the temple - day and date?
The Dwarkadhish Temple hosts several monthly events and rituals, which are integral to its spiritual activities. Here are some key monthly events, including their typical days and dates:
1. Ekadashi:
* Day: 11th day of each lunar fortnight (twice a month)
* Significance: Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, this day involves special prayers, fasting, and rituals. Devotees believe that observing Ekadashi helps in attaining spiritual purity and liberation.
2. Amavasya (New Moon Day):
* Day: New moon day of each lunar month
* Significance: Amavasya is considered an auspicious day for performing ancestral rites and rituals. Special offerings and prayers are conducted to honor ancestors and seek their blessings.
3. Purnima (Full Moon Day):
* Day: Full moon day of each lunar month
* Significance: This day is marked by special rituals and prayers. The temple holds additional poojas and ceremonies to celebrate the full moon, which is seen as a time of spiritual fullness and enlightenment.
4. Pradosh Vrat:
* Day: 13th day of each lunar fortnight
* Significance: Dedicated to Lord Shiva, Pradosh Vrat involves fasting and evening prayers. The rituals are believed to remove sins and bring peace and prosperity.
5. Sankranti:
* Day: Transition of the sun from one zodiac sign to another, typically the 14th or 15th of each month
* Significance: Each Sankranti marks a significant transition period and is considered an auspicious time for performing religious activities and making donations.
Important Annual Monthly Events:
1. Akshaya Tritiya:
* Month: Vaisakh (April-May)
* Significance: Akshaya Tritiya is considered one of the most auspicious days of the year. On this day, special rituals and poojas are performed, and the deity is adorned with flowers and sandalwood paste.
2. Tulsi Vivah:
* Month: Kartik (October-November)
* Significance: Celebrates the ceremonial marriage of Tulsi (holy basil) with Lord Vishnu. The festival starts on the 11th day of Kartik and continues for four days with various rituals.
3. Sharad Purnima:
* Month: Ashwin (September-October)
* Significance: The full moon night is celebrated with special prayers and rituals, marking the end of the monsoon season and the beginning of the harvest season.
