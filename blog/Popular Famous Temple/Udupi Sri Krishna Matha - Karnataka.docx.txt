   * The temple has three separate dining halls:
      * One for the general public where most visitors eat.
      * Exclusive areas for invited guests, Brahmins, scholars, and donors, which provide a more private dining experience.
   * The food is served on a first-come, first-served basis, and everyone is welcome to partake in the meals, reflecting the inclusive spirit of the temple.
4. Types of Food Served:
   * The meals are strictly vegetarian and are prepared in a 'satvik' manner, which means they do not contain onions, garlic, or other tamasic ingredients. Common dishes include rice, sambar, rasam, and a variety of vegetable preparations.

Construction and Scientific Information about Udupi Sri Krishna Matha
Historical and Architectural Significance:
1. Founding and Architecture:
   * Udupi Sri Krishna Matha was established in the 13th century by the Vaishnavite saint Madhvacharya. The temple is built in the Dravidian architectural style, which is prominent in South India. The construction involves intricate woodwork and stone carvings typical of the period.
2. Main Structure:
   * The temple features a sanctum sanctorum where the deity Lord Krishna is enshrined. The idol of Lord Krishna is viewed through a unique window called the "Navagraha Kitiki" (nine-holed window), designed to symbolize the nine planets in Hindu astrology. This feature allows devotees to have darshan (viewing) of the deity in a distinctive manner​ (Travel Blog | Voyager - Sandy & Vyjay)​​ (www.Pilgrimaide.Com)​.
3. Materials Used:
   * Traditional materials like granite, wood, and brass were used in the construction of the temple. The outer structure is predominantly granite, while the interior features extensive use of wood for the ceilings and support beams. The doors and windows are often adorned with brass and silver plating, adding to the temple's ornate aesthetic​ (Wikipedia)​.
Scientific and Environmental Aspects:
1. Structural Design:
   * The temple's design incorporates traditional Vastu Shastra principles, ensuring that the layout aligns with natural elements and promotes spiritual harmony. The structure is oriented in such a way that it maximizes natural light and ventilation, creating a serene and conducive environment for worship.
2. Sustainability Practices:
   * The temple complex includes a sacred tank called "Madhwa Sarovara," which is used for various religious rituals. This water body is an example of ancient water management systems, ensuring a sustainable supply of water for temple activities and maintaining ecological balance​ (Travel Blog | Voyager - Sandy & Vyjay)​.
3. Cultural and Educational Impact:
   * The temple is not only a place of worship but also a center for cultural and educational activities. It promotes the study of Dvaita Vedanta philosophy, Sanskrit, and traditional arts, ensuring the preservation and transmission of ancient knowledge systems

Annual Event at Udupi Sri Krishna Matha
Paryaya Festival
Date and Significance:
* Frequency: The Paryaya festival is held once every two years on January 18th.
* Significance: This event marks the transfer of temple management from one of the Ashta Mathas (eight monasteries) to another. The swamis of these monasteries take turns managing the temple, each for a two-year period.
Highlights of the Festival:
* Pomp and Ceremony: The festival includes grand processions, cultural performances, and religious rituals.
* Religious Observances: Special pujas and ceremonies are conducted, and thousands of devotees participate in the festivities.
* Historical Tradition: The Paryaya festival has a history of over 500 years and is a vital part of the temple's cultural and spiritual life.
Other Major Annual Festivals:
1. Krishna Janmashtami:
   * Date: Celebrated in August (date varies according to the Hindu lunar calendar).
   * Significance: Marks the birth of Lord Krishna. The temple is beautifully decorated, and special midnight prayers and celebrations are held.
2. Ratha Sapthami:
   * Date: Celebrated in February.
   * Significance: This festival is dedicated to Lord Surya (Sun God). The temple organizes a grand chariot procession during this festival.
3. Makara Sankranti:
   * Date: Celebrated in January.
   * Significance: Marks the transition of the sun into the zodiac sign of Capricorn (Makara). It is a time for new beginnings and harvest celebrations.
4. Navaratri and Vijayadashami:
   * Date: Celebrated in October.
   * Significance: Navaratri is a nine-night festival dedicated to the Goddess Durga, and Vijayadashami marks the victory of good over evil.
5. Deepavali (Diwali):
   * Date: Celebrated in October/November.
   * Significance: The festival of lights, celebrating the victory of light over darkness.
29. Monthly Event of the temple - day and date?
Monthly Event at Udupi Sri Krishna Matha
Ekadashi Celebrations
Frequency:
* Monthly Event: Ekadashi occurs twice a month according to the lunar calendar—once during the waxing phase (Shukla Paksha) and once during the waning phase (Krishna Paksha).
Significance:
* Spiritual Importance: Ekadashi is considered highly auspicious in Hinduism and is dedicated to Lord Vishnu, of whom Lord Krishna is an avatar. It is a day for fasting, prayer, and seeking spiritual growth.
* Observances: Devotees observe fasting and participate in special prayers and rituals. The temple organizes additional pujas and readings of sacred texts on these days.
Dates:
* Variable Dates: Since Ekadashi is based on the lunar calendar, the exact dates vary each month. Devotees can refer to a Hindu calendar (Panchang) for the specific dates of Ekadashi each month.
How to Participate:
* Temple Schedule: On Ekadashi days, the temple might have extended hours for darshan and special puja timings. It's advisable to check with the temple's official website or contact their administration for detailed schedules.
