Condensed Information about the Temple:
A. Main Deity:
* Sai Baba: Sai Baba of Shirdi, also known as Shirdi Sai Baba, is the primary deity worshipped at this temple. He is revered by people of all faiths and is known for his teachings of love, forgiveness, helping others, charity, contentment, inner peace, devotion to God and guru. His philosophy encapsulated the essence of both Hinduism and Islam.
B. Other Deities (Sub deities):
* Khandoba: There is a shrine dedicated to Lord Khandoba, a regional deity, within the temple premises.
* Gurusthan: A site under a neem tree where Sai Baba was first spotted meditating. It's considered a significant spot by devotees.
* Dwarkamai: The mosque where Sai Baba spent a significant portion of his life and is a place of worship within the temple complex.
* Chavadi: Another important spot where Sai Baba used to sleep every alternate night.
* Other shrines: Small shrines dedicated to Lord Ganesha, Lord Shiva, and Lord Shani are also present within the temple complex.
The temple complex also includes various facilities for devotees including accommodation, dining, and medical aid. It actively engages in various charitable activities, supporting initiatives in education, healthcare, and social welfare.
General Days:
* Opening Time: The temple opens at 4:45 AM.
* Closing Time: The temple closes at 11:00 PM, following the Shej Aarti.
Daily Schedule:
* The temple conducts four main Aartis during the day:
   * Kakad Aarti at 5:15 AM
   * Midday Aarti at 12:00 Noon
   * Dhoop Aarti at Sunset (approximately 6:30 PM to 7:00 PM depending on the time of year)
   * Shej Aarti at 10:30 PM
Special Days and Festivals:
* On Thursdays and during major festivals like Ram Navami, Guru Purnima, and Vijaya Dashami, the temple follows a similar opening and closing schedule but may have additional rituals and processions. For example, every Thursday, Baba’s palkhi procession starts at 9:15 PM from the Samadhi Mandir.

Timings:
* Food Serving Timings: 10:00 AM to 10:00 PM daily.
* Ticket Counter Timings: 9:30 AM to 9:30 PM.

2. First Floor Halls:

   * Cost: INR 50 per person for a meal ticket.
   * Features: These halls are equipped with dining tables, and there is an elevator available for easy access.

* VIP Meal Passes: Available for INR 50, offering faster service with a waiting time of less than 10 minutes.

Booking and Procedure:

* Advance Booking: While there is no online booking for meals, devotees can get their meal passes from the counters on arrival.

Historical Background:
* Origins: The Shirdi Sai Baba Temple, also known as the Samadhi Mandir, was built over the samadhi (tomb) of Sai Baba. The construction began in 1915 by a wealthy devotee, Shreemant Gopalrao Buti, and was completed in 1918, the year Sai Baba attained Mahasamadhi.
* Initial Purpose: Originally intended as a Wada (residential building) with a temple for Lord Krishna, it was converted into a Samadhi Mandir following Sai Baba's wishes and subsequent passing.
Architectural Details:
* Materials Used: The Samadhi is constructed from white marble stones, and the temple features intricate ornamental decorations. The statue of Sai Baba, seated on a throne, is made from Italian marble and was sculpted by Balaji Vasant in 1954.
* Design Elements: The temple includes a railing around the Samadhi, embellished with silver and other decorative elements. A silver umbrella is placed above Sai Baba’s statue, adding to its grandeur.
* Assembly Hall: In front of the Samadhi, there is a large assembly hall that can accommodate around 600 devotees. This hall also showcases various belongings of Sai Baba and artifacts depicting his life.
Modern Facilities and Scientific Aspects:
* Eco-Friendly Initiatives: The temple complex includes a solar-powered kitchen, one of the largest in Asia, which significantly reduces the carbon footprint and provides meals to thousands of devotees daily.
* Technological Integration: The temple employs advanced crowd management and security systems to ensure the safety and comfort of visitors. CCTV cameras and metal detectors are installed throughout the premises.
Temple Expansion and Construction Policy:
* New Projects: The Shri Saibaba Sansthan Trust is involved in constructing new Sai Baba temples across India to spread his teachings. These projects often include additional facilities like hospitals and dining halls to serve the local communities.
* Community Services: The temple trust also undertakes various social initiatives, including education and medical services, funded by donations from devotees.
Spiritual Significance:
* Samadhi Mandir: The Samadhi Mandir is the focal point of the temple complex, symbolizing peace, faith, and spiritual fulfillment. Devotees from all over the world visit the temple to seek blessings and experience the serene atmosphere.
