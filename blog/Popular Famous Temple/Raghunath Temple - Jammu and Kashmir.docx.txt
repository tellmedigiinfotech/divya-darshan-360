24. .Provide Construction/  scientific  information about the  temple 
The Raghunath Temple in Jammu is a significant architectural and spiritual site. Its construction was initiated by Maharaja Gulab Singh and completed by his son Maharaja Ranbir Singh in the 19th century. The temple showcases a blend of Mughal and Sikh architectural styles, characterized by intricate carvings and expansive courtyards.
Construction Highlights:
* Materials: Local sandstone and marble
* Design: Features multiple shrines within a large complex
* Scientific Aspects: Natural light optimization, ventilation, and structural stability suited to the region's climate
Architectural Features:
* Central Dome: Prominent feature surrounded by smaller domes
* Inner Sanctums: Contain numerous saligrams, sacred stones representing Lord Vishnu
* Courtyards: Spacious areas for large gatherings and rituals
This blend of architectural prowess and scientific design reflects the temple's importance in both historical and modern contexts.
