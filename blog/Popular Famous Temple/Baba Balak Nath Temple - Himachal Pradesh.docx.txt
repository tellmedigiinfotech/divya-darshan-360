Official Registration and Management
* Government Oversight: The management and administration of the Shri Baba Balak Nath Temple Deothsidh were taken over by the Government of Himachal Pradesh on January 16, 1987, under the H.P. "Hindu Public Religious Institution and Charitable Endowments Act, 1984."

28. Annual Event of the temple day and date?
The Baba Balak Nath Temple in Deotsidh, Himachal Pradesh, hosts several significant annual events that attract a large number of devotees from across India and abroad. Here are the key events:
1. Chaitra Mela (March-April):
   * This is the largest annual fair at the temple, celebrated from March 14 to April 14. It attracts thousands of devotees who participate in various cultural and religious activities. The fair includes continuous food distribution (langar) throughout the month and is marked by special prayers and rituals​ (Ehimachal)​​ (Hindu Blog)​.
2. Baba Balak Nath Jayanti:
   * Celebrated in January or February, this festival marks the birth anniversary of Baba Balak Nath. It is a major occasion for his followers who gather to pay their respects and seek blessings​ (Ehimachal)​.
3. Navratri:
   * This nine-day festival is celebrated twice a year, during the months of Chaitra (March-April) and Ashwin (September-October). Devotees worship the nine forms of Goddess Durga with great enthusiasm, making it a vibrant and spiritually significant period at the temple​ (Ehimachal)​.
4. Shravan Ashtami:
   * Celebrated on the eighth day of the Hindu month of Shravan (July-August), this festival involves fasting and special prayers dedicated to Baba Balak Nath. It is considered an auspicious day for his devotees​ (Ehimachal)​.
5. Guru Purnima:
   * Observed on the full moon day in the month of Ashadha (June-July), Guru Purnima honors the Guru or spiritual teacher. This day is significant for followers of Baba Balak Nath, who consider him a divine guide​ (Ehimachal)​.

29. Monthly Event of the temple - day and date?
The Baba Balak Nath Temple in Deotsidh, Himachal Pradesh, hosts several significant monthly and annual events that draw devotees from across the region and beyond.
Annual Events
1. Chaitra Mela (March-April):
   * The Chaitra Mela is the largest annual fair held at the temple from March 14 to April 14. This month-long event features cultural performances, religious rituals, and continuous food distribution (langar) throughout the period​ (Ehimachal)​​ (Hindu Blog)​.
2. Baba Balak Nath Jayanti:
   * Celebrated in January or February, this festival marks the birth anniversary of Baba Balak Nath. It is a major event where devotees gather to seek blessings and participate in special prayers​ (Ehimachal)​.
3. Navratri:
   * Navratri is celebrated twice a year, during the months of Chaitra (March-April) and Ashwin (September-October). This nine-day festival involves worship of the nine forms of Goddess Durga and is marked by special rituals and celebrations​ (Ehimachal)​.
4. Shravan Ashtami:
   * Celebrated on the eighth day of the Hindu month of Shravan (July-August), this event involves fasting and special prayers dedicated to Baba Balak Nath​ (Ehimachal)​.
5. Guru Purnima:
   * This festival honors the Guru and is celebrated on the full moon day in the month of Ashadha (June-July). Devotees pay homage to Baba Balak Nath as their spiritual guide​ (Ehimachal)​.
Monthly Events
* Sunday Celebrations:
   * Every Sunday is considered particularly auspicious at the Baba Balak Nath Temple. Devotees throng the temple in large numbers on this day to seek blessings and participate in the special prayers conducted.
