A. Main Deity
* Lord Murugan (Dhandayuthapani Swamy): The main deity worshipped in the Palani Murugan Temple is Lord Murugan, also known as Dhandayuthapani. He is depicted here in a renunciant form, carrying a staff (Danda) as his weapon, and is considered one of the most important among the six abodes of Lord Murugan (Arupadaiveedu).
B. Other Deities (Sub-deities)
* Valli and Devasena: The temple also venerates Valli and Devasena, consorts of Lord Murugan, though they are not the primary focus here.
* Other Sub-deities: The temple complex includes smaller shrines dedicated to various other deities, integrating a broad spectrum of divine figures revered in Hindu tradition.

28. Annual Event of the temple day and date?
The major annual event of the Palani Murugan Temple is Thaipusam, which is celebrated with great fervor and devotion.
Thaipusam:
* Date: Thaipusam is celebrated during the Tamil month of Thai, which typically falls in January or February. The exact date varies each year based on the Tamil lunar calendar and the alignment of the star Pusam (Pushya) with the full moon.
* Significance: Thaipusam commemorates the occasion when Goddess Parvati gave Lord Murugan the powerful Vel (spear) to vanquish the demon Tarakasura. It is a day dedicated to Lord Murugan's victory and is marked by grand processions, carrying of the kavadi, and other devotional activities.
* Activities: The festival includes a procession of Lord Murugan’s idol on a golden chariot, devotees carrying kavadis, and various special poojas and rituals performed throughout the day and night.
Key Features:
1. Kavadi Attam: Devotees carry kavadis (semi-circular, decorated structures) as a form of penance and thanksgiving. This is a significant aspect of the Thaipusam celebrations.
2. Processions: Grand processions featuring the deity on a golden chariot are a major highlight, drawing thousands of devotees.
3. Special Poojas: Numerous special poojas and abhishekams (ritual bathing) are conducted throughout the day.
Other Notable Events:
* Panguni Uthiram: Celebrated in the Tamil month of Panguni (March-April), marking the divine marriage of Lord Murugan with Goddess Deivanai.
* Skanda Shashti: A six-day festival in Aippasi (October-November), commemorating Lord Murugan’s victory over the demon Surapadman.


29. Monthly Event of the temple - day and date
The Palani Murugan Temple observes several monthly events and special poojas. One of the most significant monthly events is the Karthigai Pooja.
Karthigai Pooja:
* Day: The Karthigai Pooja is observed every month on the day of Karthigai Nakshatram (star).
* Significance: Karthigai is considered an auspicious day dedicated to Lord Murugan. Devotees believe that worshipping Lord Murugan on this day brings prosperity and removes obstacles.
* Activities: Special poojas, abhishekams (ritual bathing of the deity), and offerings are performed. Devotees light lamps and participate in the pooja ceremonies with great devotion.
Other Monthly Observances:
* Sashti Viratham: Observed on the sixth day after the new moon (Amavasya) every month. This day is dedicated to Lord Murugan and involves fasting and special poojas.
* Krithigai: Celebrated on the day when the Krithigai star aligns, involving special rituals and offerings to Lord Murugan.
Timings and Details:
* The exact timings and dates of these monthly events vary based on the Tamil lunar calendar. It is advisable to check the temple's official website or contact the temple administration for precise dates each month.
