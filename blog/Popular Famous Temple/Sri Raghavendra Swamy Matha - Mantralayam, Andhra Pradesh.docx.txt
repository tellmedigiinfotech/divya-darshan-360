Construction and Scientific Information about Sri Raghavendra Swamy Matha
Architectural Features:
1. Design:
   * The temple exhibits traditional South Indian architecture with intricate carvings, pillars, and sculptures.
   * The main shrine houses the Brindavana of Sri Raghavendra Swamy, which is a beautifully carved structure made of stone.
2. Materials:
   * The primary construction materials include granite and marble, ensuring durability and aesthetic appeal.
3. Layout:
   * The temple complex includes multiple mandapas (halls) for various rituals and ceremonies, spacious courtyards, and accommodation facilities for devotees.
Scientific Aspects:
1. Ventilation and Natural Lighting:
   * The temple is designed to allow ample natural light and ventilation, creating a serene and comfortable environment for visitors.
2. Structural Integrity:
   * The use of granite and other sturdy materials ensures the structural integrity of the temple, allowing it to withstand natural elements over centuries.

Annual Event at Sri Raghavendra Swamy Matha
Aradhana Mahotsavam:
* Event: Annual celebration commemorating the life and teachings of Sri Raghavendra Swamy.
* Date: The exact date varies each year based on the Hindu lunar calendar, typically falling in the month of Shravana (July-August).
* Activities: Special poojas, abhishekams, spiritual discourses, and cultural programs.
29. Monthly Event of the temple - day and date?
Monthly Event at Sri Raghavendra Swamy Matha
Punya Thithi:
* Event: Commemoration of Sri Raghavendra Swamy's monthly Punya Thithi (sacred remembrance day).
* Date: Observed on the day of the star Arudra (Thiruvathira) every month as per the Hindu lunar calendar.
* Activities: Special poojas, abhishekams, and devotional singing.
