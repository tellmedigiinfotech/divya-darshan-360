5.        Location- How to Reach/commuting-  Flight/Train/Bus/Taxi Stand/Ferry/Port Air- what ever is available?
Reaching Yamunotri Temple, Uttarakhand
1. Air Travel:
* The nearest airport to Yamunotri is the Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun, approximately 210 kilometers away. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Hanuman Chatti, the starting point for the trek to Yamunotri​ (FirstTrek)​.
2. Rail Travel:
* Dehradun Railway Station is the closest major railway station, well-connected to various major cities in India. From here, taxis and buses are available to Hanuman Chatti​ (FirstTrek)​.
3. Road Travel:
* If traveling by road, the journey to Hanuman Chatti involves driving through Mussoorie and Barkot. Hanuman Chatti is the last motorable point from where the trek to Yamunotri starts. You can hire taxis or buses from major cities like Dehradun or Rishikesh to reach Hanuman Chatti​ (FirstTrek)​.
4. Trekking:
* From Hanuman Chatti, pilgrims typically trek the remaining 6 kilometers to the temple. Ponies and palanquins are available for hire for those who might need assistance for the trek. The trek offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas and is a vital part of the spiritual journey to Yamunotri​ (FirstTrek)​.
5. Local Transport:
* Local taxis and buses are available from Dehradun or Rishikesh to Hanuman Chatti. From there, pilgrims either trek or hire ponies to reach the temple. During the pilgrimage season, shared jeeps and minibuses also operate frequently along this route​ (FirstTrek)​.

24. .Provide Construction/  scientific  information about the  temple 
Construction:
* The Yamunotri Temple is an ancient shrine dedicated to Goddess Yamuna, situated in the Garhwal Himalayas at an altitude of approximately 3,293 meters.
* The temple structure is made of stone and features typical North Indian architectural elements, including a conical roof and intricate carvings.
* It was originally built by Maharaja Pratap Shah of Tehri Garhwal and has been renovated several times due to damage from natural elements.
Scientific Information:
* The temple is located near the Yamunotri glacier, which is the source of the Yamuna River.
* The region is known for its geothermal activity, with hot springs like Surya Kund near the temple, where devotees cook rice as an offering to the deity.
