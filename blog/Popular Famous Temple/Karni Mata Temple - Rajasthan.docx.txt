28. Annual Event of the temple day and date?
The Karni Mata Temple in Deshnok, Rajasthan, hosts the Karni Mata Fair twice a year. These events are significant annual occurrences, drawing large numbers of devotees. The first fair takes place during the Navratras in March-April, and the second occurs during the Navratras in September-October. The fairs celebrate and honor Karni Mata, a revered figure in the region believed to possess supernatural powers and who dedicated her life to the service of the poor.
The temple itself, a stunning structure of stone and marble, becomes the focal point of these fairs. Pilgrims and tourists visit to participate in rituals, offer prayers and prasad, and seek the blessings of Karni Mata. The presence of sacred rats, believed to bring luck, adds a unique element to the temple atmosphere during these fairs.
For more specific dates and details about the events or to plan a visit during these fairs, you may want to check closer to the event months or consult local resources.
29. Monthly Event of the temple - day and date?
The Karni Mata Temple in Deshnok, Rajasthan, hosts two significant annual fairs which are the most prominent events at the temple. These fairs occur twice a year, aligning with the Navratri festivals:
1. The first fair is held during the Chaitra Navratri, starting from Chaitra Shukla Ekam to Chaitra Shukla Dashmi, which generally falls around March-April.
2. The second fair takes place during the Ashvin Navratri, from Ashvin Shukla Ekam to Ashvin Shukla Dashmi, typically occurring in September-October.
These fairs draw thousands of devotees who participate in various rituals, offer prayers and seek blessings from Karni Mata, viewed as a form of Goddess Durga. The temple's unique aspect, besides the religious significance, is the presence of sacred rats, considered to be the sons of Karni Mata, adding a unique element to the spiritual experience.
