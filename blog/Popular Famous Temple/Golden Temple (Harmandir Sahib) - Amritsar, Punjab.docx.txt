Annual Events at the Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib)
The Golden Temple hosts several significant annual events and festivals that attract pilgrims and visitors from around the world. Here are some of the key events:
1. Vaisakhi (April 14th): Vaisakhi, celebrated on April 14th, marks the Sikh New Year and commemorates the formation of the Khalsa by Guru Gobind Singh in 1699. This day is celebrated with great fervor at the Golden Temple, featuring religious processions, special prayers, and community meals.
2. Gurpurab Celebrations: The birthdays of Sikh Gurus, known as Gurpurabs, are celebrated with immense devotion. The most notable Gurpurabs are:
* Guru Nanak Dev Ji's Birthday (November): The founder of Sikhism, Guru Nanak Dev Ji's birth anniversary, usually falls in November. The temple is beautifully illuminated, and the celebrations include continuous recitation of the Guru Granth Sahib, kirtan (devotional singing), and langar (community meals).
* Guru Gobind Singh Ji's Birthday (January): The tenth Sikh Guru, Guru Gobind Singh Ji's birthday, is celebrated in January with similar devotion and grandeur.
3. Martyrdom Days:
* Martyrdom of Guru Arjan Dev Ji (June): The martyrdom of Guru Arjan Dev Ji, the fifth Sikh Guru, is observed in June. Devotees engage in special prayers and kirtans to honor his sacrifice.
* Martyrdom of Guru Tegh Bahadur Ji (November-December): The martyrdom of the ninth Sikh Guru, Guru Tegh Bahadur Ji, is observed in November or December with prayers and remembrance ceremonies.
4. Diwali (October-November): Diwali, the Festival of Lights, is celebrated with great enthusiasm at the Golden Temple. The temple is adorned with lights and decorations, and a spectacular fireworks display is held. The celebrations also include special prayers and langar.
5. Hola Mohalla (March): Hola Mohalla is a unique Sikh festival celebrated a day after Holi, usually in March. It features displays of martial arts, mock battles, and kirtan, along with community meals and religious processions.

Monthly Events at the Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib)
While the Golden Temple primarily hosts several grand annual events, there are also regular monthly events and practices that offer visitors a chance to engage in the temple's spiritual activities. Here are some of the key monthly events:
1. Amavasya (New Moon) Observance: Amavasya, or the New Moon day, is observed every month with special prayers and kirtan. Devotees gather to seek blessings and reflect on spiritual teachings. The date varies each month based on the lunar calendar.
2. Sangrand (Sankranti) Celebrations: Sangrand marks the beginning of a new month according to the Nanakshahi calendar (Sikh calendar). It is a day for special prayers and kirtan at the Golden Temple. Sangrand typically falls around the 14th or 15th of each Gregorian month, though the exact date can vary slightly.
3. Puranmashi (Full Moon) Kirtan: On the night of Puranmashi, or the Full Moon, the Golden Temple holds an extended session of kirtan (devotional singing). This is a time for reflection and spiritual immersion. The date varies each month based on the lunar calendar.
4. Weekly Akhand Path: Every month, an Akhand Path (continuous reading of the Guru Granth Sahib) is held over the course of a week. This event involves the uninterrupted recitation of the entire Guru Granth Sahib, which typically takes about 48 hours. The Akhand Path is a significant spiritual practice and is scheduled at least once a month.
5. Special Langar Services: In addition to daily Langar, the Golden Temple organizes special Langar services on significant monthly occasions such as Sangrand and Puranmashi. These services emphasize the Sikh principles of selfless service (Seva) and community support.
For specific dates of these monthly events, you can refer to the Golden Temple events calendar or check with the temple's official announcements.
