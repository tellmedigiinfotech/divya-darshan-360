A. Main Deity:
* Goddess Chamunda (Chamundeshwari):
   * Goddess Chamunda is a form of Durga, worshipped as a fierce aspect of the divine feminine. She is considered the goddess of war and is often depicted slaying demons.

* Lord Shiva (Nandikeshwar):
   * In the temple complex, Lord Shiva is worshipped alongside Goddess Chamunda. The temple is also known as Chamunda Nandikeshwar Dham, indicating the presence of Nandikeshwar (another name for Lord Shiva).

Chamunda Devi Temple - Special Poojas and Rituals
Chamunda Devi Temple offers various special poojas and rituals that devotees can participate in. Here are the details regarding the days, timings, rates, and booking procedures for these poojas:
Special Poojas and Rituals:
1. Daily Poojas:
   * Aarti:
      * Morning Aarti: 5:00 AM
      * Evening Aarti: 7:00 PM
   * Abhishekam: A ritual bathing of the deity with sacred substances like milk, honey, and water, performed daily.
2. Special Poojas:
   * Chandi Path:
      * Description: A special recitation of the Durga Saptashati, invoking the blessings of Goddess Chamunda for health, prosperity, and protection.
      * Days: Usually performed on Tuesdays and Fridays, considered auspicious for Devi worship.
      * Timings: Typically in the morning around 9:00 AM.
      * Rates: Approximately ₹1500 - ₹3000, depending on the number of recitations and the scale of the ritual.
   * Navratri Poojas:
      * Description: Special poojas conducted during the Navratri festival, including elaborate rituals, havans (fire sacrifices), and continuous recitation of scriptures.
      * Days: During the nine days of Navratri (dates vary each year based on the lunar calendar).
      * Timings: Throughout the day, with main rituals in the morning and evening.
      * Rates: Special packages available, ranging from ₹5000 to ₹15000, depending on the specific rituals included.
   * Havan (Fire Sacrifice):
      * Description: A sacred fire ritual performed to invoke the blessings of the gods and purify the surroundings.
      * Days: Can be booked on request, commonly performed on special occasions and festivals.
      * Timings: Usually performed in the morning.
      * Rates: Approximately ₹2500 - ₹5000, depending on the scale of the havan.
   * Kumari Pooja:
      * Description: A ritual where young girls (representing the goddess) are worshipped and offered gifts. This pooja is believed to bring blessings and good fortune.
      * Days: Commonly performed during Navratri and other special occasions.
      * Timings: Morning or early evening.
      * Rates: ₹1000 - ₹2000.
Advance Booking Procedure:
1. Online Booking:
   * Temple Website: Some poojas can be booked in advance through the temple’s official website. Navigate to the booking section, select the desired pooja, choose the date, and complete the payment process.

2. On-Site Booking:
   * Temple Office: Visit the temple office or the designated counter within the temple premises to book poojas in person. It is advisable to book at least a day in advance, especially for special poojas.
