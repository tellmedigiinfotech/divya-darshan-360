Talakaveri Temple is considered especially significant because it marks the origin point of the River Kaveri, which is revered as a holy river in South India. The temple is situated at the Brahmagiri hill, which according to Hindu mythology, is where the river begins from a spring at a location identified by a tank. This site holds immense spiritual importance as devotees believe that the waters of the Kaveri have the power to cleanse sins and help attain salvation. The temple attracts pilgrims especially during Tula Sankramana, when the waters are said to gush forth at a predetermined moment.
