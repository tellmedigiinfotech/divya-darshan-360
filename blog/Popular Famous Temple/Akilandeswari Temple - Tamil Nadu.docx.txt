Construction and Scientific Information about Akilandeswari Temple (Thiruvanaikoil)
Architectural Significance:
1. Historical Background:
   * The Akilandeswari Temple, also known as Jambukeswarar Temple, is a significant example of Dravidian architecture. Major Annual Events and Festivals:

   * Description: This is one of the most significant festivals celebrated at the temple. Panguni Uthiram is dedicated to the celestial marriage of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati.

   * Description: Fridays in the Tamil month of Aadi (July-August) are considered highly auspicious. Special poojas and rituals are performed for Goddess Akilandeswari.

3. Navarathri Festival:
   * Description: Navarathri, a nine-day festival dedicated to Goddess Durga, is celebrated with great fervor. Special poojas and cultural programs are held.
   * Date: This festival usually falls in September-October.
4. Aipasi Annabishekam:
   * Description: Aipasi Annabishekam is a special abhishekam (ritual bath) performed with cooked rice. It is a significant event dedicated to Lord Shiva.
   * Date: This event falls on the full moon day in the Tamil month of Aipasi (October-November).
5. Vaikasi Visakam:
   * Description: Vaikasi Visakam is celebrated with special poojas and rituals in honor of Lord Muruga.
   * Date: This festival occurs in the Tamil month of Vaikasi (May-June).
6. Mahashivaratri:
   * Description: Mahashivaratri is a major festival dedicated to Lord Shiva, observed with night-long vigils, special poojas, and abhishekams.
   * Date: This festival usually occurs in February-March.
29. Monthly Event of the temple - day and date?
Regular Monthly Events:
1. Pradosham:
   * Description: Pradosham is a significant bi-monthly occasion dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is observed on the 13th day (Trayodashi) of both the waxing (Shukla Paksha) and waning (Krishna Paksha) moon phases. Special poojas and rituals are performed in the evening.
   * Days: Twice a month, on Trayodashi (13th day) of each lunar fortnight.
   * Timings: Generally in the evening, around sunset.
2. Sankatahara Chaturthi:
   * Description: This day is dedicated to Lord Ganesha and is observed on the fourth day after the full moon (Krishna Paksha Chaturthi). Devotees perform special poojas to seek the removal of obstacles and to gain blessings.
   * Days: Once a month, on the fourth day after the full moon.
   * Timings: Usually in the evening.
3. Pournami (Full Moon Day):
   * Description: The full moon day is considered highly auspicious, and special poojas and rituals are performed for both Lord Shiva and Goddess Akilandeswari.
   * Days: Once a month, on the full moon day.
   * Timings: Throughout the day, with special rituals often conducted in the evening.
4. Amavasya (New Moon Day):
   * Description: The new moon day is also considered significant, with special poojas dedicated to Lord Shiva and other deities.
   * Days: Once a month, on the new moon day.
   * Timings: Throughout the day, with special rituals in the evening.
